# Taste extraction itself: can five minutes carry the Orchestra?

**Workstream H — PostListener → The Orchestra**

The most upstream question in this thesis has a defensible answer: **PostListener's current profiling extracts enough between-participant signal to drive audibly differentiated ElevenLabs output, but only if we stop defending it as a psychometric instrument and start defending it as a *felt-personalization scaffold*.** The AVD model is empirically real (three replications across ratings, behaviour, and self-report) but is genre-free, culturally narrow, and explains under 10% of personality-linked preference variance — so it was never going to be the precision instrument the thesis hinted at. What it *is*, combined with a five-minute interactive instrument that triggers IKEA-effect ownership and Barnum-effect recognition, is an engine for producing music that six different participants will hear as six different pieces and experience as theirs. The remainder of this report defends that claim empirically, diagnoses where the current profiling wastes its signal, and specifies the minimum set of fixes needed before the ElevenLabs pilot and June 2026 defence.

The stakes: Workstream A showed felt recognition leans on Barnum/Forer, IKEA, and personalization-placebo mechanisms. Workstream B showed differentiation needs three orthogonal macro dimensions to clear perceptual thresholds. Workstream H now confirms that the bottleneck is not the AVD model itself but (a) a Spectrum phase that wastes 7 of 8 pairs on a single axis, (b) a demo-mode short-circuit that ignores the vector entirely, and (c) a missing autobiographical module that carries the highest ratio of evidence-to-effort of any improvement on the table.

## 1. The AVD model — what it actually measures and whether it's sufficient

**AVD is a genuine empirical structure, not a convenient fiction, but it is a narrow one.** Greenberg, Kosinski, Stillwell, Monteiro, Levitin, and Rentfrow (2016, *Social Psychological and Personality Science*) derived the three dimensions from 76 judges rating 102 excerpts across 26 genres on 38 attributes. A principal-components analysis with varimax rotation (KMO = .87) recovered three components explaining **73% of variance** in perceived attributes, replicating within rock-only and jazz-only subsamples.

The facet content is specific and citable. **Arousal** loads positively on *intense* (.94), *tense* (.92), *forceful* (.88), *aggressive* (.86), *abrasive* (.84), *thrilling* (.81), and negatively on *mellow* (−.81), *gentle* (−.80), *calming* (−.75). **Valence** loads positively on *happy* (.87), *fun* (.86), *merry* (.82), *joyful* (.81), *lively* (.76), and negatively on *depressing* (−.85), *sad* (−.57). **Depth** loads positively on *intelligent* (.89), *sophisticated* (.84), *inspiring* (.82), *complex* (.66), *poetic* (.66), *deep* (.65), *thoughtful* (.60), and negatively on *party music* (−.57), *danceable* (−.54). These loadings should appear in the thesis appendix verbatim — they are what the profiling ostensibly measures.

Orthogonality is partial. Varimax rotation guarantees zero correlation between factor *scores*, but in the underlying hierarchical solution **Arousal is the cleanest dimension, while Valence and Depth share meaningful latent variance**. Grüning et al. (2024) confirmed this pattern in the TAP validation: Arousal–Valence overlap substantially; Valence–Depth overlap least. The practical consequence for PostListener is that a participant high on Valence is modestly more likely to be low on Depth (party-danceable pole), and this weak coupling should be acknowledged rather than hidden behind independence claims.

**Fricke, Greenberg, Rentfrow, and Herzberg (2021, *Psychology of Music*, 49(3), 371–381)** — the 1M-people / 204,317-song streaming study — is the ecological-validity cornerstone. Running principal-components analysis on psychological and ESSENTIA-extracted sonic features from behavioural Million Song Dataset listening, they recovered the three-factor AVD structure from unobtrusive behaviour, not self-report. A companion paper (Fricke et al., 2018, *Journal of Research in Personality*, 75, 94–102) reported computer-extracted features correlating with human ratings on the MUSIC model at **mean r = .88** — a correspondence that matters because it closes the loop: PostListener can *measure* generated output in AVD space using the same ESSENTIA pipeline that validated the model.

**The TAP instrument (Grüning, Fricke, Rentfrow & Greenberg, 2024, preprint)** operationalises AVD as 18 items across 9 facets: Arousal (Energetic, Calming, Aggressive), Valence (Happy, Sad, Danceable), Depth (Emotional depth, Cerebral, Mystical). Reported hierarchical CFA fit is **CFI = .856, RMSEA = .087, SRMR = .105** — acceptable for a three-level factor structure but **below the conventional .90/.95 cut-off for good fit**. The preprint emphasises factor loadings over Cronbach's alpha because two items per facet cap α mechanically. This is citable as psychometric support but not as evidence of a polished instrument — and it matters that TAP itself sits near the edge of conventional fit thresholds.

**Personality correlations are real but small.** In Greenberg et al. (2016) Study 2 (N = 9,454), Openness correlated positively with both Valence and Depth; Extraversion's domain-level correlation with Arousal was a tiny r = −.05 but its *excitement-seeking facet* correlated r ≈ .20 with Arousal; Agreeableness associated negatively with Arousal and positively with Depth; Neuroticism positively with Arousal, negatively with Valence. **Total R² for personality predicting each AVD dimension is under 10%.** The popular-press framing that music taste reveals personality overstates this badly. For the thesis, the honest line is: AVD captures a replicable structure of *musical attribute perception*; it captures personality only faintly.

**What AVD misses is not marginal.** It is genre-agnostic (the same AVD coordinates can be reggaeton, ambient, or gamelan), era-agnostic (a 1950s rock track and a 2020s hyperpop track at identical AVD land in different listening worlds), function-agnostic (Schäfer, Sedlmeier, Städtler & Huron, 2013, *Frontiers in Psychology*, 4, 511, showed listening functions form a separate three-factor space of self-awareness, social relatedness, and arousal/mood regulation), lyric-agnostic, and culturally narrow (Greenberg et al., 2022, *JPSP*, 122(2), found cross-cultural variation in a 53-country extension). Critically, **AVD was built on deliberately unfamiliar stimuli, so it excludes nostalgia and familiarity by construction** — which means a preference model descended from it *structurally cannot* capture the autobiographical dimension of musical identity.

For the Orchestra, three dimensions are **necessary but not sufficient as the sole generation-conditioning space**. Chill lo-fi, ambient drone, and solo piano ballad sit at near-identical AVD coordinates yet are categorically different listening experiences. Genre, era, and functional use must ride alongside AVD, either explicitly from the profiling or implicitly from an archetype anchor (Section 4).

## 2. What the current profiling phases actually extract — and what they miss

**Spectrum is the most broken phase by a wide margin.** Thurstonian paired-comparison measurement (Thurstone, 1927; Brown & Maydeu-Olivares, 2011, *Educational and Psychological Measurement*, 71, 460–502) requires **a minimum of two items per latent dimension for model identification and ≥3 for stable estimation**, balanced on social desirability. Simulation work (Maydeu-Olivares, 2003) establishes adequate parameter estimates with seven items per dimension. The current Spectrum allocates **seven pairs to Valence and effectively zero pairs to Arousal and Depth** (because both sides of pairs 1–7 carry identical A and D coordinates). The consequence is formally precise: **Arousal and Depth scores from Spectrum are not merely noisy but underdetermined; reliability is undefined, not low**. Whatever A and D signal the final AVD vector contains comes entirely from DepthDial (for D) and Moment tapping (for A, with the caveats below). Spectrum is currently a Valence-only instrument masquerading as a three-dimensional one.

**DepthDial is defensible as a single-slider instrument but only barely.** Wanous, Reichers, and Hudy (1997, *Journal of Applied Psychology*, 82, 247–252) meta-analysed 28 single-item correlations (N = 7,682) and reported mean uncorrected r = .63 with multi-item equivalents, corrected r = .67, with a **minimum reliability estimate of .70 for global unidimensional constructs**. Kuhlmann, Dantlgraber, and Reips (2017, *Behavior Research Methods*) found visual analog sliders yield comparable reliability to Likert scales (N = 879). The catch: Depth is *not* cleanly unidimensional — Greenberg's loadings split it into cerebral sophistication and emotional profundity. A single slider compresses these, and the thesis should acknowledge that DepthDial probably operates near the .70 reliability floor, which is acceptable for formative profiling and inadequate for criterion-grade measurement.

**Dwell and reaction time carry real signal but not the signal the current code assumes.** Stillman, Medvedev, and Ferguson (2020, *PNAS*, 117(29)) showed mouse-tracking area-under-curve predicts risk preferences beyond choice outcomes themselves; Stillman, Shen, and Ferguson (2018, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*) position hesitation as indexing **decisional conflict between near-equal utilities**, not preference strength. The thesis's implicit assumption that long dwell = strong preference is backwards: long dwell usually means conflict. The correct weighting scheme is to **standardise log-RT within participant, down-weight choices with RT > +1.5 SD or trajectory reversals, and treat faster/straighter choices as higher-confidence contributions to the latent score**. PostListener currently weights none of this, so dwell signal is not extracted at all.

**Textures should absolutely migrate from synthesised Web Audio to ElevenLabs clips at known AVD coordinates.** Nave, Minxha, Greenberg, Kosinski, Stillwell, and Rentfrow (2018, *Psychological Science*, 29(7), 1145–1158) established that **15-second audio excerpts elicit preference structure that predicts Big Five with external validity superior to genre self-report**. Krumhansl (2010, *Music Perception*) showed listeners identify popular songs from 400-ms clips; 10–15s is ample for preference without fatigue. Poetic descriptors ("sacred," "electric") are vulnerable to semantic idiosyncrasy and social-desirability bias documented since Rentfrow and Gosling (2003). The ElevenLabs Textures upgrade is not a nice-to-have; it is a methodologically defensible replacement of a weaker probe with a stronger one, and is the single best item to frame in the ElevenLabs funding proposal.

**Moment's tapping-as-Arousal mapping is partly valid but conflates constructs.** Janata, Tomic, and Haberman (2012, *JEP: General*, 141(1), 54–75) established that groove ratings are reliably inter-subjectively consistent, correlate strongly with enjoyment, elicit spontaneous rhythmic movement, and are quantitatively indexed by tap periodicity and tap-to-beat asynchrony. Madison (2006) and MacDougall and Moore (2005) located preferred spontaneous tapping near ~120 BPM. However, Hurley, Martens, and Janata (2014) and Witek, Clarke, Wallentin, Kringelbach, and Vuust (2014) document that **groove, liking, and difficulty collapse into a single enjoyment factor**, so tapping rate indexes arousal *compounded with valence and liking*. Greenberg's Arousal is about perceived sonic/rhythmic intensity of the *stimulus*; Moment measures the *participant's motor engagement*. These are related but not identical. Treating Moment output as a composite engagement/arousal signal (not a pure A score) and pairing it with a one-question liking rating at the end is the theoretically honest fix. **The halved normalisation constants in Moment.jsx are indefensible without documentation and should either be reverted or justified with a calibration dataset.**

**The missing dimension — musical autobiography — has the strongest evidence-to-effort ratio of any addition.** Krumhansl and Zupnick (2013, *Psychological Science*, 24(10), 2057–2068) showed that preference, recognition, quality judgments, and emotional response all **peak for music released during a listener's ages 14–22**, with cascading reminiscence bumps extending to parental-generation music. Janata, Tomic, and Rakowski (2007, *Memory*, 15(8), 845–860) showed ~30% of popular-music excerpts spontaneously evoke autobiographical memories with positive-valence bias, and Janata (2009, *Cerebral Cortex*, 19(11), 2579–2594) localised music-evoked autobiographical memory to medial prefrontal cortex — a neurally grounded construct entirely absent from AVD. Bonneville-Roussy, Rentfrow, Xu, and Potter (2013, *JPSP*, 105(4), 703–717) tracked >250,000 participants across the lifespan, showing preferences are stable trait-like but systematically age-shifted, meaning biographical anchoring carries variance orthogonal to current-state AVD. **A two-minute autobiographical module — "name one song from your younger self, one song for right now, one song you'd want played at a milestone moment" — would add genre, era, and identity anchoring that the current profile structurally cannot provide**, and it does so at implementation cost measured in days.

On the trait-vs-state-vs-history question, the empirical verdict is clear: **a hybrid trait + history design has the strongest support**. Trait framing is backed by Rentfrow and Gosling (2003), Rentfrow et al. (2011), Nave et al. (2018), and the 53-country replication (Greenberg et al., 2022). Pure-state framing is weakest — day-to-day mood preference is noisy and a poor predictor of identity. History framing has the strongest evidence for stability and personal meaningfulness (Krumhansl & Zupnick, 2013; Janata, 2007, 2009; Jakubowski et al., 2020). The current PostListener sits awkwardly in pure-state territory ("what do you respond to right now in these stimuli?"). Moving it to trait + history is the single most important theoretical shift in this workstream.

## 3. The "enough signal" question — minimum viable profiling for felt personalization

**The commercial evidence is unambiguous: three to ten explicit signals are enough to bootstrap felt personalization.** Spotify's onboarding asks for a small number of artists, genres, and languages; in a 2025 published ablation, removing onboarding signals dropped nDCG@50 by 13.8% on onboarding-aligned clusters — meaningful but bounded, confirming that these signals *seed* rather than *determine* personalization. De Pauw, Ruymbeek, and Goethals (2025, *Scientific Reports*) built an active-learning recommender constrained to exactly **three likes and no more than twenty queries** based on estimated user patience and demonstrated measurable recommendation gains. Pandora's Music Genome Project bootstraps stations from a single seed track plus thumbs feedback. Netflix uses a handful of initial genre selections. **No commercial system relies on psychometrically valid trait estimation; all of them rely on a plausible seed followed by behavioural refinement.** PostListener's 5 minutes is generous by this standard.

**Felt personalization does not require accurate personalization.** The Barnum/Forer effect (Forer, 1949, *Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology*, 44, 118–123; Snyder, Shenkel, and Lowery, 1977, *J. Consulting and Clinical Psychology*, 45, 104–114; Furnham and Schofield, 1987) shows people accept generic feedback as uniquely self-descriptive, with effect amplified by positive valence, high-status source, and the belief that the feedback comes from a legitimate instrument. Layne (1979) showed ratings of bogus and real inventory feedback are equivalent — subjective validation does not distinguish them. **Kosch et al. (2022, *ACM TOCHI*) replicated this for adaptive AI interfaces**: participants told they were using an adaptive system reported higher performance expectations and satisfaction despite receiving identical non-adaptive content. Li (2016, *Computers in Human Behavior*) established the same "placebo effect in web-based personalization." The Spotify Wrapped literature (Annabell & Vindum Rasmussen, 2025, *New Media & Society*; Obiegbu & Larsen, 2025) documents users feeling "seen" by personality tags with negligible psychometric validity. **I could not locate a published 2×2 "actually personalized × told personalized" study in AI-generated music — this is an open empirical gap and a defensible novel contribution for the thesis.**

**Effort produces ownership.** Norton, Mochon, and Ariely (2012, *Journal of Consumer Psychology*, 22(3), 453–460) demonstrated across four studies that self-assembly increases valuation of the result, with boundary conditions that **completion is required** and **modest effort suffices**. Franke, Schreier, and Kaiser (2010) generalised the effect to product configuration; Jin et al. (2019) showed users given initial control over genre selection expressed higher confidence in recommender output independent of actual quality. The implication: a five-minute instrument that is genuinely engaging and reliably finishable will produce more felt personalization than a thirty-minute passive behavioural log.

**The perceptual thresholds are macro, not micro.** Drake and Botte (1993) report tempo just-noticeable differences of 6–10% for non-musicians; Friberg and Sundberg (1995) converge on ~4.4% for non-musicians at 100 BPM. Pitch JND is ~0.5% of frequency (~3–10 cents for trained listeners). These fine-grained thresholds are **irrelevant to felt personalization**. Listener-level discrimination is dominated by genre, instrumentation, vocal presence, tempo class, and mood — macro features. A 10 BPM shift, a major-to-minor shift, or a synth-to-acoustic instrumentation swap is supra-threshold for essentially everyone; a two-cent pitch shift is not. **Two AVD vectors differing by 0.2 on one axis will produce audibly different generation only if that 0.2 delta crosses a macro threshold (e.g., tempo class boundary, mode boundary, archetype boundary)**. This observation drives the mapping architecture in Section 4.

**Short instruments capture substantial between-person variance even when internal consistency is low.** The Ten-Item Personality Inventory (Gosling, Rentfrow & Swann, 2003) has low Cronbach's α per trait — two items cannot produce high homogeneity — but acceptable 6-week test-retest and mean convergent r ≈ .54 with longer BFI/NEO. It is "best used as a quick screening tool rather than for high-stakes decisions." Combining TIPI-style traits with STOMP-style genre factors and 3 artist picks produces a profile space on the order of 10^6+ plausible combinations — vastly more variance than six ElevenLabs archetype generations can exploit. **The binding constraint on PostListener is not inter-participant variance in the profile; it is inter-output variance in the generator.**

**The honest synthesis: PostListener should defend felt personalization, not psychometric validity.** The thesis cannot credibly claim both, and trying produces contradictions (AVD is a publishable instrument + we'll halve the Moment normalisation constants + we'll skip the Moment.jsx generation call in demo mode). The coherent position is: AVD is a defensible but imperfect preference scaffold; the five-minute instrument extracts enough between-participant variance to drive macro-feature-differentiated generation; the Barnum, IKEA, and placebo effects convert "different enough" output into "made for me" experience; the thesis's empirical contribution is a design that orchestrates these mechanisms at spatial-audio scale.

## 4. What the AVD vector should actually map to in the generation pipeline

**ElevenLabs' Music API exposes no numeric mood sliders**, only a free-text `prompt` or a structured `composition_plan` JSON, plus `music_length_ms`, `force_instrumental`, `model_id`, `output_format`, and `seed`. ElevenLabs' own best-practices documentation confirms the model responds to both abstract mood descriptors ("eerie") and detailed musical language, honours explicit BPM and key statements, and honours `solo <instrument>` and `a cappella` keywords. Prompt length does not always correlate with quality. The AVD vector must therefore be **rendered into prose at generation time** — there is no parameter sidecar.

**Audio-feature mapping per dimension** is grounded in Eerola and Vuoskoski (2011, *Psychology of Music*, 39(1), 18–49; 2013, *Music Perception*, 30(3), 307–340) and Juslin and Laukka's (2003, *Psychological Bulletin*, 129(5), 770–814) meta-analysis of 145 studies on emotional expression cues.

| AVD axis | Acoustic correlates | Prompt-layer rendering |
|---|---|---|
| **Arousal** | Tempo (BPM), rhythmic density, spectral centroid, dynamic range, onset rate, loudness | Low (0–.2): 50–70 BPM, pp, dark, legato. Mid (.5–.8): 100–130 BPM, mf–f, bright, driving. High (.8–1): 130–180 BPM, ff, high centroid, percussive 16ths |
| **Valence** | Mode (major/minor), harmonic consonance/dissonance, melodic contour, articulation | Low (0–.3): minor, dissonant, melancholic/mournful. Mid (.3–.6): modal, suspensions, wistful/bittersweet. High (.6–1): major, consonant, warm/joyful |
| **Depth** | Harmonic complexity, timbral diversity, structural sophistication, production dynamic range | Low (0–.3): diatonic, programmed drums, polished radio mix. Mid: extended chords, band+extras. High (.85–1): modal interchange, counterpoint, chamber/orchestral, wide-stereo audiophile mix |

**Depth is the acoustically noisiest dimension** because it conflates structural complexity and aesthetic weight. The thesis should consider explicitly defining Depth operationally (e.g., "arrangement complexity + production audiophile-ness") rather than inheriting the latent construct.

**Genre must be a required fourth input, not derived from AVD.** Text-to-music models are trained on caption-audio pairs where genre is the dominant stylistic anchor. ElevenLabs, Suno, and MusicGen prompt guides uniformly list genre as the first prompt element. Rentfrow et al. (2012, *Music Perception*, 30(2), 161–185) showed the same AVD region can be rendered in radically different genres listeners experience as categorically different. **A pure AVD prompt without genre will collapse to the model's default (typically generic cinematic rock)**. Genre must come from the profiling — either an explicit genre selection, a seed artist/track mapped via Spotify categories or Every Noise At Once, or an archetype default (below).

**The continuous-vs-discrete question resolves to a hybrid.** Pure continuous AVD cannot guarantee audible differentiation because deltas under ~0.2 sit within generator stochasticity; ElevenLabs' own docs note that identical seed+prompt only approximates reproducibility. Pure discrete archetypes lose within-cluster granularity. The defensible architecture is **six canonical archetypes supplying genre palette, instrumentation, and production style, with continuous AVD modulating BPM, mode, and adjective intensity on top**:

| # | Archetype | Anchor (A, V, D) | Genre palette | Instruments |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mellow-Contemplative | (.2, .5, .8) | Ambient, neo-classical, jazz ballad | Felt piano, strings, Rhodes |
| 2 | Euphoric-Pop | (.8, .9, .3) | Dance-pop, house | Synth stabs, 4-on-floor |
| 3 | Intense-Dark | (.9, .2, .5) | Industrial, trap, drill | Distorted guitar, 808s |
| 4 | Sophisticated-Melancholic | (.3, .25, .9) | Chamber, modern classical, art-rock | String quartet, clarinet |
| 5 | Unpretentious-Warm | (.4, .75, .35) | Folk, country, acoustic pop | Acoustic guitar, brush kit |
| 6 | Contemporary-Groove | (.7, .6, .45) | R&B, hip-hop, funk | Rhodes, bass, syncopated drums |

This mirrors how Pandora bootstraps stations from seeds and how Spotify's Taste Communities operate: discrete anchor + continuous refinement. It guarantees that any two participants assigned to different archetypes will hear categorically different output, satisfying Workstream B's three-macro-dimension differentiation requirement, while preserving within-archetype AVD modulation for within-archetype variety.

**The recommended prompt-mapping specification** converts (A, V, D, genre_anchor, duration) into an ElevenLabs prompt via five steps: (1) archetype lookup via tertile quantisation to nearest anchor; (2) continuous modulation — `bpm = 55 + 110 × arousal`, `mode = "minor" if V<.45 else "major"` with modal band .45–.60, dynamic and complexity descriptors keyed to A and D respectively; (3) template rendering `"{genre}, {bpm} BPM, in {key/mode}, {dynamic}, featuring {instruments}, {complexity_descriptor}, {valence_adjective} mood, {mix}, {instrumental_flag}"`; (4) for >60s runs, use `composition_plan` JSON to map AVD to section-level arcs with a peak-A chorus and peak-D bridge; (5) fix `seed` per user for within-session coherence and enforce **Δ ≥ 0.2 per axis** as the minimum for within-user variant auditioning.

Worked example. For (A=0.75, V=0.25, D=0.85, genre="post-rock"): *"Post-rock, 135 BPM, in E minor, loud and driving, featuring tremolo electric guitar, cello, and crashing drums; extended chords with modal interchange and a gradual crescendo; mournful, foreboding mood; dynamic, wide stereo, audiophile mix; instrumental only."* This is a single-call rendering of a single AVD point and maps cleanly into the Orchestra's composition-plan architecture.

## 5. Honest assessment, prioritised modifications, and a pilot protocol

### The current profiling's strengths and weaknesses

**Strengths.** The six-phase structure is a genuinely good interaction design for a five-minute window, exploiting multiple modalities (binary choice, continuous slider, audio response, motor behaviour) that trigger IKEA-effect ownership and break up the monotony that kills short-instrument completion rates. Anchoring on Greenberg's AVD gives the thesis a psychometrically legitimate backbone with three published validations (Greenberg 2016, Fricke 2019, Grüning 2024) — stronger empirical grounding than Spotify Wrapped's personality tags and comparable to any peer-reviewed preference instrument of similar length. The eventual ElevenLabs Textures upgrade is a genuine methodological improvement over Web Audio synthesis.

**Weaknesses.** Spectrum pairs 1–7 are a silent bug: **Arousal and Depth receive effectively zero signal from seven of eight Spectrum pairs**, which means the entire A and D axes of the final vector are being estimated from a single Spectrum item plus DepthDial's slider plus Moment's tap-conflated-engagement score. Moment's tapping mapping conflates arousal with valence and liking per Janata's groove literature, and the halved normalisation constants are undocumented. Demo mode's `Promise.resolve('/chamber/tracks/track-a.mp3')` in `Moment.jsx` **silently bypasses the entire AVD-to-generation pipeline**, so the current system cannot in principle produce felt personalization — every demo participant hears the same track regardless of profile. The phases are entirely current-state-framed, missing the autobiographical dimension that carries the strongest evidence for stable personal musical identity. The doc/code drift on the Result phase is symptomatic of the deeper problem that the profiling's theoretical frame has drifted from its implementation.

### Prioritised modifications for June 2026

The ranking below is by (impact on felt personalization) × (1 / implementation effort). Items 1–3 are non-negotiable; 4–6 are high-leverage improvements; 7–8 are defensible additions that can defer.

1. **Fix demo mode's generation short-circuit (1–2 days, critical)**. Replace `Promise.resolve('/chamber/tracks/track-a.mp3')` with the actual AVD-to-prompt rendering from Section 4. Without this fix, no pilot can validate any other claim in this workstream — the profile currently doesn't drive anything.

2. **Rebalance Spectrum to at least three heteropolar pairs per dimension (2–3 days, critical)**. Current allocation is effectively 1/7/0 on A/V/D; target 3/3/2 or 3/2/3 on an 8-pair instrument, or expand to 9 pairs at 3/3/3. Pairs must vary on *all three* AVD coordinates, not just Valence, and must be balanced on social desirability. Without this fix, Arousal and Depth scores from Spectrum are psychometrically undefined.

3. **Revert or document Moment normalisation constants and add a liking probe (1 day, critical)**. Either restore the spec constants with calibration evidence or publish the revised constants with a rationale. Add one post-Moment 7-point liking rating on the tapped stimulus to disambiguate groove's conflation of arousal with valence and liking per Hurley et al. (2014).

4. **Ship the ElevenLabs Textures upgrade (1–2 weeks, high impact)**. Replace Web Audio synthesis with ElevenLabs clips pre-positioned at known AVD coordinates. Nave et al. (2018) establishes audio excerpts as methodologically superior to abstract descriptors; this is the single strongest funding-proposal argument to ElevenLabs because it demonstrates their model in the instrument itself.

5. **Add a two-minute autobiographical module before Reveal (3–5 days, high impact)**. Three prompts: one song tied to the participant's younger self (with age-at-release captured for cascading-bump coding per Krumhansl & Zupnick 2013), one song for right now, one song for a future milestone. The three selections supply genre anchors (Section 4 requires this) and an era vector. This is the highest-ratio addition in the plan.

6. **Weight Spectrum choices by confidence, not preference, using dwell time (2–3 days, moderate impact)**. Implement within-participant log-RT standardisation, down-weight RT > +1.5 SD, use straightness/reversals as additional confidence modifiers per Stillman et al. (2020). Do *not* interpret long dwell as strong preference.

7. **Cluster participants into six archetypes at Reveal and map to genre palettes (1 week)**. Implement the six-archetype hybrid mapping (Section 4). This guarantees categorical differentiation across participants and mirrors Pandora/Spotify practice.

8. **Restore the Result phase or document its removal (0.5 day)**. Doc/code drift is a thesis-defence liability.

### AVD-to-ElevenLabs composition plan specification

The composition plan produced by the profiling has six fields: `archetype_id` (1–6), `arousal`, `valence`, `depth` (continuous [0,1]), `genre_anchor` (string from autobiographical module or archetype default), and `era_hint` (string from autobiographical bump-year analysis). The Orchestra's sixteen-minute structure uses `composition_plan` JSON with section-level AVD arcs: the conducting phase holds the participant's central AVD coordinate ±0.1 modulation; the dissolution phase drifts systematically toward the archetype's complementary pole (e.g., a high-A participant experiences gradual decrescendo to low-A ambient textures). Each section is rendered with the prompt template from Section 4, fixed `seed` per participant, `force_instrumental` from a pre-Orchestra toggle, and Δ ≥ 0.2 enforced between consecutive sections to maintain perceptual differentiation.

### The three-framing decision

The profiling should adopt an explicit **trait-plus-history** framing, dropping the current ambiguous state framing. Trait elements: the AVD extraction from Spectrum, DepthDial, Textures, and Moment (defensibly preference-trait under Greenberg's framework, which treats AVD as a disposition). History elements: the new autobiographical module (formative song, current song, milestone song), which supplies era, genre, and identity anchors absent from AVD itself. State framing is explicitly *excluded* — participants are not asked what they feel right now but what they orient toward across time. This is empirically cleaner (Bonneville-Roussy et al. 2013 preference stability evidence), theoretically coherent with Workstream A's felt-ownership goals (autobiographical memory activation drives medial prefrontal self-reference per Janata 2009), and practically implementable.

### The clustering-vs-continuous decision

The pipeline should use **six discrete archetypes + continuous AVD modulation within archetype**, not pure-continuous and not pure-discrete. Pure-continuous cannot guarantee audible differentiation at AVD deltas under 0.2; pure-discrete loses within-cluster granularity and breaks the thesis's claim that profile differences drive output differences. The hybrid mirrors commercial practice (Pandora seed-plus-thumbs, Spotify archetype-plus-refinement), and provides a clean evaluation story: archetype differentiation is the macro-feature claim (genre, instrumentation), AVD modulation is the within-archetype claim (BPM, mode, dynamics). Both can be tested in the pilot.

### Minimum viable pilot protocol

Recruit **eight to twelve participants**, stratified to vary age (span at least 20 years), musical training (mix of Gold-MSI lay and trained), and genre exposure (not all rock/pop listeners). Each participant completes the full revised profiling end-to-end, yielding (AVD vector, archetype assignment, genre anchor, era hint). For each participant, generate three ElevenLabs outputs: **P1** = their own profile fully rendered; **P2** = a shuffled profile from another participant with a *different archetype* (macro-differentiation test); **P3** = a perturbed version of their own profile with AVD noise of ±0.15 on two axes (fine-differentiation test, *below* the recommended 0.2 threshold — deliberate null).

Participants rate all three outputs in randomised order on four 7-point scales: felt-for-me, liking, recognition-of-identity, and perceived effort of the system. Between-participant AVD-vector variance is logged as the profile-spread check (target: first two principal components of the AVD vector pool span at least 60% of the theoretical cube volume; if spread collapses to a narrow band, the instrument has a restriction-of-range problem). Primary outcome: felt-for-me(P1) > felt-for-me(P2), p < .05 with even this small N if the effect size is substantial; ceiling effects across all three P conditions would indicate Barnum dominance over actual personalization. Secondary outcome: felt-for-me(P1) ≈ felt-for-me(P3), confirming that Δ < 0.2 is subliminal and defending the 0.2 threshold as the operating granularity. Run time per participant: ~35 minutes. Budget: a single week of ElevenLabs API credits plus recruiting.

This pilot is the minimum credible defence for the ElevenLabs funding proposal. If P1 > P2 on felt-for-me with real effect size, the thesis has empirical evidence that PostListener produces felt personalization mediated by genuine profile differences. If the result is null (P1 = P2 = P3), the thesis can still defend the Barnum/IKEA framing but must acknowledge that actual profile differences are not doing the work — an honest result that remains publishable as a design-research finding.

## Conclusion

The profiling extracts enough. Not perfectly — the Spectrum phase currently throws away most of its Arousal and Depth signal, the Moment phase conflates arousal with liking, and demo mode actively bypasses the vector it just computed — but the underlying design, once fixed at the three critical points, produces a three-number-plus-autobiographical-anchor profile that can drive six categorically different ElevenLabs archetypes and within-archetype AVD modulation at a 0.2-per-axis operating granularity that beats the macro-level perceptual threshold without requiring psychometric precision the AVD model doesn't have anyway.

The deeper move this workstream forces is framing discipline. The thesis must stop hedging between "psychometrically valid preference instrument" and "felt-personalization scaffold" and defend the second explicitly, because the evidence supports the second and because the first would require a multi-year validation programme PostListener cannot run before June 2026. Greenberg's AVD is real but small. Spotify Wrapped's emotional effect is big but fake. The Orchestra sits between these and needs the scaffolding of both: enough real signal from the profiling to drive genuine macro-differentiation across participants, plus enough Barnum, IKEA, and placebo framing for participants to convert that differentiation into the phenomenology of "made for me." That is a defensible graduation thesis and a coherent funding ask. The novel empirical contribution — the 2×2 actual × told pilot that does not yet exist in the AI music literature — is the wedge that makes this workstream not just a cleanup operation but a contribution.

The single most important thing to implement first is the demo-mode fix. Everything else is secondary to the fact that the current pipeline cannot, in principle, produce felt personalization because it does not in fact generate different music for different people. Fix that, rebalance Spectrum, add the autobiographical module, ship Textures on ElevenLabs, run the eight-participant pilot. That is Workstream H made real, and it is achievable in the time remaining before submission.
