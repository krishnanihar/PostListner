# PostListener: Literature synthesis and 5-minute taste-extraction redesign

This memo grounds the redesign of PostListener's 5-minute profiling phase in the strongest available academic and practitioner literature, then proposes an opinionated, phase-by-phase flow that captures specific emotions (not just AVD), genre/era/aesthetic, and ritual function — all mapping cleanly to a 24-track handpicked Suno library. The headline argument: **the existing AVD-only Spectrum cannot recover nostalgia, awe, or tenderness as distinct states; a hybrid pairwise-audio + GEMS-aligned discrete probe + autobiographical bump module + Forer mirror, scaffolded inside a Van Gennep separation rite, is the literature-supported design**. Final output is a 6-archetype × 4-variation library with a hierarchical knowledge-based match (Pandora-lite) + ε-greedy variation-level serendipity.

## SECTION 1 — LITERATURE SYNTHESIS

### 1A. Music preference elicitation instruments

**Genre labels are the wrong primitive.** Rentfrow & Gosling's STOMP (2003, *JPSP* 84:1236) introduced the four-factor genre model (Reflective/Complex, Intense/Rebellious, Upbeat/Conventional, Energetic/Rhythmic), but the same group later acknowledged that genre labels are *stereotype proxies*: "rock" subsumes Beatles, Hendrix, and contemporary indie, conflating demographic identity with sonic preference. Greenberg, Kosinski, Stillwell, Monteiro, Levitin & Rentfrow (2016, *SPPS* 7:597) replaced genre labels with audio-elicited factor structure: 102 pieces × 38 attributes → 3 factors (AVD), explaining ~73% of variance. This is PostListener's current model.

**Audio-based elicitation is materially more predictive.** The canonical empirical defence is Nave, Minxha, Greenberg, Kosinski, Stillwell & Rentfrow (2018, *Psychological Science* 29:1145): N=22,252 rated **25 unfamiliar 15-second excerpts** spanning the MUSIC model (Rentfrow, Goldberg & Levitin 2011, *JPSP* 100:1139). Audio reactions predicted Big Five out-of-sample at r ≈ 0.16–0.21 — meaningfully exceeding STOMP-only baselines for traits beyond Openness. Fricke, Greenberg, Rentfrow & Herzberg (2018, 2019) replicated AVD structure from 1M users / 200K songs of behavioural exposure data, confirming the same latent geometry holds across audio ratings, computed features (mean r = 0.88 between human and audio-feature ratings), and behavioural traces. The Saari & Eerola programme (Saari et al. 2013, ISMIR; 2014, *IEEE TKDE*) shows audio is more efficient than tags for predicting valence — the historically hardest dimension.

**How few trials suffice.** The cold-start / active-learning literature converges on **15–30 well-chosen forced-choice trials** for stable low-dimensional preference recovery. Golbandi, Koren & Lempel (2010, CIKM) showed a depth-6 decision tree (~6 questions) materially improves Netflix-style cold-start RMSE. Rashid et al. (2002, IUI) found gains plateau by ~20 ratings. Recent advances — Nguyen et al. (2024, arXiv:2406.00973), Salem et al. (2025, arXiv:2510.27342) — confirm pairwise (Bradley-Terry) is more sample-efficient than Likert and that **embedding-based personalization works with 10–20 ratings** when paired with adaptive selection. Midjourney's 200-pair onboarding (V7, mandatory April 2025) is comfortably above the information-theoretic minimum (~d log k pairs for rank-d embedding); Midjourney has not published its rationale, but the figure functions as much as IKEA-effect ritual as as preference signal.

**Stimulus design.** Eerola & Vuoskoski (2011) and Nave et al. (2018) used 15-second excerpts; Cowen et al. (2020) recovered 13 stable emotion dimensions from 5-second clips. **Fifteen seconds is the defensible minimum** for global affective judgement; unfamiliar excerpts isolate stylistic from autobiographical preference. For a browser instrument, fatigue caps trial count at ~25–30 brief judgements at 10s/trial.

**Recent advances.** CLAP (Elizalde et al. 2022, arXiv:2206.04769; Wu et al. 2023, arXiv:2211.06687), MuLan (Huang et al. 2022, arXiv:2208.12415), MERT (Li et al. 2023, arXiv:2306.00107), and Jukebox-as-feature-extractor (Castellon, Donahue & Liang 2021, ISMIR) make text↔audio similarity a one-line operation. Behavioural signals (skip, replay, dwell) are now standard in industry.

### 1B. Discrete and domain-specific music emotion models

**The strongest argument against AVD-only profiling is geometric.** AVD cannot disambiguate nostalgia from peacefulness (both low-arousal, positive-valence) or awe from generic high-arousal positive — yet these are exactly the emotions PostListener wants to evoke. Eerola & Vuoskoski (2011, *Psych. Music* 39:18) showed dimensional models lose information at category boundaries; Vuoskoski & Eerola (2011) found GEMS shows strongest sensitivity to individual differences while dimensional models show highest inter-rater consistency. **Dimensions are reliable; categories are sensitive.**

**GEMS** (Zentner, Grandjean & Scherer 2008, *Emotion* 8:494; combined N>800 across four studies): 515 emotion terms → 45 items → **9 first-order factors** under three superordinate clusters — **Sublimity** (wonder, transcendence, tenderness, **nostalgia**, peacefulness), **Vitality** (power, joyful activation), **Unease** (tension, sadness). GEMS was a domain-specific response to the limits of basic-emotion and circumplex models. Categories explicitly excluded as not endorsed for music: shame, guilt, disgust, anger-as-fight, embarrassment, pride. Short forms: GEMS-25, GEMS-9. Jacobsen, Strauss, Vigl, Zangerle & Zentner (2025, *Musicae Scientiae*; N=192) showed GEMS-9 profiles correlate strongly in shape with GEMS-45 and take ~1 minute per excerpt — **operationally relevant for a 5-minute instrument**.

**GEMIAC** (Coutinho & Scherer 2017, *Music Perception* 34:371): 14-class checklist (paired affect terms — "enchanted, in awe"; "nostalgic, sentimental"; "full of tenderness, warmhearted"; "moved, touched"), explicitly designed for **<2 minutes per excerpt** rapid assessment. Closest ready-to-deploy short instrument that admits emotion blends.

**Cross-cultural validity.** Cowen, Fang, Sauter & Keltner (2020, *PNAS* 117:1924) — "What music makes us feel" — collected 2,168 5-second samples rated by US (n=1,591) and Chinese (n=1,258) participants and recovered **13 cross-culturally preserved dimensions**: amusement, joy, eroticism, beauty, relaxation, sadness, dreaminess, triumph, anxiety, scariness, annoyance, defiance, "pumped up." Crucially, **categorical feelings such as "triumphant" were better preserved across cultures than valence/arousal**, and "awe" emerged as a stable category dissociable from joy. Cowen does **not** surface nostalgia — likely because 5-second unfamiliar clips lack autobiographical hook (Barrett et al. 2010, *Emotion* 10:390). For PostListener, **nostalgia must be elicited via familiar / user-supplied seeds or explicit verbal probes**, not unfamiliar audio.

**Specific-emotion operationalisation.** Nostalgia: Barrett et al. (2010) showed it is strongest when songs are autobiographically salient, arousing, familiar, and elicit mixed affect. Awe: Keltner & Haidt (2003, *Cognition & Emotion* 17:297) — appraisal of vastness + need-for-accommodation; Pilgrim, Norris & Hackathorn (2017) found awe in music is distinct from joy and predicted by openness + need-for-cognition. Chills: Salimpoor, Benovoy, Larcher, Dagher & Zatorre (2011, *Nature Neuroscience* 14:257) — endogenous striatal dopamine release; caudate during anticipation, NAcc at peak; pleasurable music vs neutral t(49)=25.0, p<.001. Pleasurable sadness: Sachs, Damasio & Habibi (2015, *Front Hum Neurosci* 9:404); Taruffi & Koelsch (2014, *PLOS ONE*; N=772) identify multiple distinct rewards from sad music — nostalgia, peacefulness, tender longing, no-real-life-consequences — supporting a separate "sweet-sad/longing" cluster.

**The minimum item set** that recovers GEMS individually is ~9 items × 3–5 well-spanned excerpts (profile-correlation r≈0.7–0.85 vs GEMS-45). Pairwise forced-choice ("which made you feel more nostalgic?") is more sample-efficient than Likert.

### 1C. Autobiographical music memory and identity

The autobiographical module is described in project files as the highest evidence-to-effort addition. The literature strongly supports this.

**The reminiscence bump for music is one of the most replicated effects in autobiographical memory.** Krumhansl & Zupnick (2013, *Psych Science* 24:2057): 62 Cornell undergrads rated Billboard hits 1955–2009; found expected bump for own-teens music **plus a cascading peak for parents' bump-period (~1980–84)** — childhood home listening leaves enduring traces. Jakubowski, Eerola, Tillmann, Perrin & Heine (2020, *Music & Science* 3:1) — N=470, ages 18–82 — peak autobiographical salience and familiarity at **age 14**; Renwick & Woolhouse (2023, *Psychology of Music*) confirmed across 4,824 participants from 102 countries that age-first-heard peaks 16–20. Toiviainen et al. (2025, *Memory*) found the cascading bump diminishes with age and is "nearly absent in participants over 45" — **digital natives have more dispersed bumps**, an important caveat for the autobiographical module.

**The neural anchor is mPFC.** Janata (2009, *Cerebral Cortex* 19:2579): 30-second excerpts during fMRI in 13 participants pre-screened for ≥30% MEAM rate; **dorsal mPFC (BA 8/9) activity parametrically tracked autobiographical salience and toroidal tonality**. Critically Janata's mPFC is the canonical hub for self-referential processing. Belfi, Karlan & Tranel (2018, *Psychomusicology*) — lesion patients with mPFC damage showed internal-detail ratio of 0.28 vs 0.60 in controls (p<.001) for music-evoked memories, but were unimpaired for face-cued memories — **causal evidence that music's identity-binding power depends specifically on self-system circuitry**.

**Eliciting MEAMs efficiently.** Janata, Tomic & Rakowski (2007, *Memory* 15:845) — ~30% of song presentations evoke an MEAM in healthy young adults; majority of songs elicit emotion (positive dominant, nostalgia third). Self-selected songs produce *much denser* memories than experimenter-selected (Platz et al. 2015; Rathbone et al. 2017, *Memory & Cognition* 45:137). **Three prompts is the empirically supported minimum** (Rathbone used 5; Jakubowski 2025 confirms small sets yield reliable individual profiles). Highest-yield prompt frames per Janata (2007) social/situational taxonomy and Rathbone's "I am…" identity work: (i) song tied to a specific person, (ii) song tied to a specific place/period, (iii) song tied to a discovery moment ("first song that felt like it was yours").

**Era-specific stylistic markers.** Salakka et al. (2021, 2024) found pulse strength, brightness (spectral centroid), and rhythmic similarity to youth music predict autobiographical salience, mediated by emotional intensity. Belfi et al. (2024, *PLOS ONE*) showed Spotify-API audio features predict MEAM phenomenology — low-energetic/high-acoustic songs evoked memories rated more "calm, romantic, sad" and more vivid. North & Hargreaves (1999, 2000) frame music for adolescents as **"badge of identity"** — era-stylistic markers (gated reverb, grunge timbre, trap snare) trigger not just memory but the social position one occupied during the imprinting period.

### 1D. Ritual design and somatic onboarding

**Van Gennep's separation phase** (1909/1960) is *"symbolic behavior signifying the detachment of the individual or group … from an earlier fixed point in the social structure"* — produced via threshold-crossing, costume, silence, fasting, renaming. Turner (1969, *The Ritual Process*) extended this to **liminality** as anti-structure ("betwixt and between") and later (1974) distinguished mandatory **liminal** from voluntary **liminoid**. PostListener is squarely liminoid: designed liminality entered through a browser link.

**The Buell & Norton labour illusion** (2011, *Management Science* 57:1564): five experiments showing operational transparency → perceived effort (β=0.23) → reciprocity (β=0.58) → perceived value (β=0.68); even when participants were told exact labour amounts, transparent conditions still produced higher value perceptions. Users *prefer longer waits to instant returns when work is visible*. Direct implication: PostListener should **show its work** during the 5-minute profile — surface the AI's listening, the palette assembly, the mapping process.

**Janet Cardiff's signature moves** (Forty Part Motet 2001; audio walks): opening silence; audible breath as scene-setting before content; second-person address ("Walk with me…"); instructions to look/feel; footsteps entrained to the listener's. KQED's framing of *Forty Part Motet*: the piece "begins with the first collective breath of the virtual choir" — breath foregrounded *before* pitch.

**Parasympathetic shift is achievable in seconds.** Bernardi, Porta & Sleight (2006, *Heart* 92:445; N=24): 2-minute silent pauses between music drove heart rate, BP, and minute ventilation **below baseline** — **silence outperforms calming music**. Lehrer & Gevirtz (2014, *Front Psychol* 5:756): resonance-frequency breathing (~6 bpm) produces dramatic HRV amplitude increases "often achievable within a fraction of a minute." Zaccaro et al. (2018, *Front Hum Neurosci* 12:353) systematic review: slow breathing (<10 bpm) increases HRV, parasympathetic tone, theta/alpha EEG, positive affect — measurable in single sessions of a few minutes. The 30-second body-arrival window is at the lower edge but real.

**Punchdrunk's *Sleep No More*** (analysed in MIT/Punchdrunk *Between Worlds*, 2014) deploys a 10–15 minute van-Gennep onboarding: hidden venue (physical threshold), bag-check + phone-check (stripping ordinary identity), winding maze + Manderley Bar (liminal antechamber), mask donning (anonymisation + silencing), elevator solo-drop (atomisation). The MIT report's central finding: **online audiences who skipped these acclimatisation rituals had markedly less immersive experiences** — direct evidence that digital onboarding without somatic separation rites fails.

**ASMR** (Barratt & Davis 2015, *PeerJ* 3:e851; Barratt, Spence & Davis 2017, *PeerJ* 5:e3846): primary triggers are whispering, personal attention, crisp sounds, slow movements; **background music inhibits the sensation** — so if PostListener wants ASMR-adjacent intimacy, the first 30 seconds should *not* be under a music bed.

**The 30-second body-arrival toolkit** (synthesised): naming sensations (Critchley et al. 2004 interoception); one slow exhale (Lehrer & Gevirtz vagal afferent activation); mild physical commitment (hand on chest — embodied analogue of mask-donning); eye-closing or soft gaze; address by name + second-person framing (Janata 2009 mPFC self-engagement); ambient drone or single sustained tone *not* music; explicit threshold statement ("for the next sixteen minutes you are not your inbox"); operational transparency.

### 1E. Felt personalization vs actual personalization

**Li (2016, *Computers in Human Behavior* 54:25)** is the canonical reference: three experiments showed a personalized message can be perceived as non-personalized and vice versa; **"perceived personalization is the real driver of favorable personalization effects."** Algorithmic match is a moderating antecedent, not the cause. Tam & Ho (2006, *MIS Quarterly* 30:865) operationalised the mechanism via the Heuristic-Systematic Model: self-reference cues (the user's name) operate as peripheral heuristics. Sundar's **agency model** (2008; Sundar & Marathe 2010, *HCR* 36:298) explains the IKEA-adjacent inflation: when the user perceives self-as-source, ownership and identity-fit are inflated regardless of content quality.

**The placebo literature is even stronger than the personalization literature.** Vaccaro, Huang, Eslami, Sandvig, Hamilton & Karahalios (2018, *CHI*) showed feed-control settings that did *nothing* (random reshuffles) significantly increased reported satisfaction; users engaged in sensemaking and **blamed themselves** when the placebo controls failed. Hsu et al. (2025, *CHI*) replicated with smaller effects in ecologically-valid feeds. Kosch, Welsch, Chuang & Schmidt (2023, *ACM ToCHI* 29:56; N=369): **fake AI difficulty-adapter elevated both expectations and measured task performance**. Kloft et al. (2024, *CHI*): the placebo persists even when the AI is described negatively. Pataranutaporn, Liu, Finn & Maes (2023, *Nature Machine Intelligence* 5:1076; N=310): identical GPT-3 bot primed as "caring" vs "manipulative" — caring prime drove both ratings *and* linguistic content of the user's side of conversation in the predicted direction. **Belief about AI is mostly self-fulfilling.**

**Forer/Barnum.** Forer (1949, *J Abn & Soc Psych* 44:118): 39 students rated identical generic feedback at 4.26/5 mean accuracy. Furnham & Schofield (1987) review establishes the moderators: **(i) belief that analysis is personalized to them; (ii) high-status assessor (psychologist, scientist, computer/AI); (iii) positive or balanced statements; (iv) vague double-headed phrasing; (v) procedural opacity / mystery**. Snyder & Shenkel (1977) showed *making users do something* (a "test") before delivering generic feedback amplifies acceptance. Glick, Gottesman & Jolton (1989) — astrology skeptics show full Barnum acceptance under personalisation framing; **belief in the domain is not required**. **No peer-reviewed study tests Barnum statements specifically about musical taste** — PostListener could make a small original contribution here.

**IKEA effect.** Norton, Mochon & Ariely (2012, *J Consumer Psych* 22:453): builders willing to pay 63% more for self-assembled furniture; mechanism is competence-signalling (Mochon, Norton & Ariely 2012, *IJRM*). Boundary condition: **labour must result in successful completion**; failed builds wipe out the effect. Buechel & Janiszewski (2014, *JCR* 40:960) — structured task scaffolding produces more attachment than open-ended free design. Atakan, Bagozzi & Yoon (2014) — participation in *both* design and realisation maximises attachment. The IKEA effect descends from Festinger (1957) and Aronson & Mills (1959, severity-of-initiation paradigm).

**Pseudo-scientific tools.** Co-Star demands minute-precision birth time (Furnham specificity-of-procedure moderator) and frames itself with NASA data (high-status authority). Output stylistic moves: second-person address, three-bucket structure (Power/Pressure/Trouble — Forer balance), specific-but-unfalsifiable imagery, named glyphs/archetypes, mildly aggressive copy tone. The Pattern adds social comparison ("Bonds") and "Time Travel" retroactive readings (motivated-cognition confirmation loop). BuzzFeed quizzes complete at 96% rate on sponsored runs (Digiday 2014); shareability mechanisms (Berger 2013) — social currency, self-disclosure dopamine (Tamir & Mitchell 2012, *PNAS*), self-serving bias. **Common design moves: second-person address, flattery + mild challenge, specific-but-unfalsifiable imagery, named archetypes, authority-laundering, procedural elaborateness, aesthetic cohesion.**

**Midjourney V7 (April 2025).** Mandatory ~200-pair preference task before unlocking generation; **5 minutes**; produces a portable `--p` profile code. The pairwise primitive is RLHF-standard (Christiano et al. 2017, NeurIPS) — better-calibrated than Likert, exploits choice-supportive bias (Mather, Shafir & Johnson 2000), compounds 200 micro-IKEA-deposits. Suno V5.5 "My Taste" (March 2026) is the opposite design: passive, ambient, behavioural-signal-based; users anecdotally report "couldn't tell the difference between personalized and non-personalized." **The contrast directly informs PostListener: a one-shot 16-minute experience cannot rely on ambient learning; it must front-load felt-personalization with a Midjourney-style ritual.**

**The "CHI 2023 paradox" (n=492)** could not be located precisely. The closest documented findings make the same design point: Adomavicius, Bockstedt, Curley & Zhang (2022, *MIS Quarterly* 46:627) — personalized predicted ratings *bias* post-consumption preference; Ragot, Martin & Cojean (2020, *CHI EA*) — AI-labeled artwork rated lower than identical human-labeled (a label *nocebo*); Aguirre, Mahr, Grewal, de Ruyter & Wetzels (2015, *J Retailing* 91:34) — **covert** data collection + personalized ad → click-through *drops*. Synthesis: **the "personalized" label helps when data provenance is consensual and the user has agency, hurts when collection is covert or cultural priors about the label are negative**.

### 1F. Profile-to-library matching for small fixed catalogs

**The 24-track catalog inverts the standard recommender problem.** Collaborative filtering fails: there is no rating matrix, items are too few for latent factors, and every user is in permanent new-community mode (Bobadilla, Ortega, Hernando & Gutiérrez 2013, *Knowledge-Based Systems* 46:109). The relevant tradition is **knowledge-based recommendation** — Burke (2002, *UMUAI* 12:331), defined as systems that reason from explicit case data about items + a user query, requiring no user history. Burke's *FindMe* family and *EntreeC* hybrid are the closest precedents. Burke's tie-breaking observation directly applies: when multiple items are equivalent under the similarity metric, the system effectively picks randomly — **finer discrimination requires more knowledge, not more algorithm**.

**Pandora's Music Genome Project is the canonical fixed-catalog precedent.** ~450 hand-coded "genes" per song (rock 150, hip-hop 350, classical 450), rated 0–5 in half-integer increments by trained musicologists at ~20–30 minutes per song. Matching is weighted distance in n-dimensional gene space (US Patent 7,003,515). For a 24-track library, **fully Pandora-grade hand-coding is ~12 hours of work — entirely feasible for a thesis** and produces interpretable explanations ("selected because you indicated calm/introspective mood and prefer warm analog production").

**Embeddings.** CLAP (Wu et al. 2023; LAION-CLAP achieves GTZAN zero-shot 71%, ESC50 90.14%) and MERT (Li et al. 2023; SOTA on 14 MIR tasks, available 95M and 330M HuggingFace) make text↔audio similarity trivial. Briand et al. (2024, arXiv:2409.08987) found pretrained embeddings underperform end-to-end CF for large catalogs but **excel in cold-start / content-based regimes**. For 24 tracks, embeddings are overkill, opaque to thesis defence, and culturally biased — use only as a sanity-check second opinion if open-text input is collected. Essentia (Bogdanov et al., ISMIR 2013; Essentia.js — Correya et al., ISMIR 2020) runs in the browser and exposes named features (BPM, danceability, key, mood classifiers) that map directly to design dimensions.

**Decision precision.** A 24-way decision needs only **log₂(24) ≈ 4.58 bits**; a 10-dimensional moderate-granularity profile supplies 30+ bits — far in excess of decision capacity. The challenge is principled information collapse, not extraction. **Hierarchical decision** (archetype → variation) is interpretable, debuggable, aligned with how the library is constructed; risk is error-compounding across levels. **Flat nearest-neighbour** is smooth across the manifold but requires careful per-dimension weighting. Burke's **cascade hybrid** — hierarchical pruning to 4–6 candidates, NN tie-break — is the strongest synthesis.

**Confidence and serendipity.** Guo, Pleiss, Sun & Weinberger (2017, *ICML*; arXiv:1706.04599) established that modern neural networks are poorly calibrated and introduced **temperature scaling** — single-parameter softmax recalibration. For PostListener, T can be set heuristically to keep median session entropy moderate. **ε-greedy stochasticity at ε ≈ 0.10–0.15** balances exploitation with serendipity. Anderson, Maystre, Anderson, Mehrotra & Lalmas (2020, *WWW*; >100M Spotify users) — algorithmic diversity boosts retention more than precise exploitation; serendipity is unexpectedness × usefulness (Zhang et al. 2012, *WSDM*). TikTok-style Thompson sampling (Russo et al. 2018) is an inferred, not confirmed, mechanism; Yan et al. (2025, *ITM Web of Conferences*) found "moderate exploration strategies yield the highest effectiveness."

### 1G. Generative AI music personalization, 2024–2026

**Suno V5.5 (March 2026)** released three personalization features: Voices (verified voice clone), Custom Models (fine-tune on ≥6 user songs), and **My Taste** (free, passive — learns from prompt history, surfaces in the Style Augmentation magic wand). Mechanism is undisclosed; commercial users report subtle, often imperceptible effects. **Endel** (Berlin, 2018) takes biometric inputs (heart rate, time of day, weather, light, motion) into the patented Endel Pacific node-based engine, fixed at A=440 Hz; claims "up to 7×" focus improvement from a 2022 Arctop EEG study (industry-funded; methodological details require scrutiny). **Spotify Wrapped 2024** introduced an AI Podcast (NotebookLM/Gemini-based, two synthetic hosts), AI DJ Wrapped (Premium), and AI Playlist (natural-language → playlist) across 184 markets — **felt personalization at scale is delivered via narrative/voice framing layered on top of an existing recommender, not via per-listener model fine-tuning**.

**Microsoft Sonora** (De La Torre, Hernandez, Wilson & Amores 2025, *CHI*; DOI 10.1145/3706598.3713316; N=32 between-subjects) — voice-driven creation of 3D spatialized audio worlds via GPT + audio diffusion + Unity3D. Higher entertainment than passive control. **State-anxiety subgroup analysis (n=13/condition with moderate-to-high trait anxiety) showed significant anxiety reduction p<0.001**; low-anxiety subgroup p=0.570. Trait-anxiety stratification matters — personalization effects concentrate in users whose state has room to move. Methodologically the most directly comparable evaluation template for PostListener.

**Liminal** (Lin, Z. 2026, IRCAM Forum Workshops, Paris/Enghien-les-Bains, 18–21 March) — interactive audiovisual installation explicitly inhabiting the **liminal interaction space between human controller and AI autonomous generator**. Gestures function as contextual information that influences probabilities and trajectories rather than commanding outputs; the system maintains its own behavioural coherence. Authorship is "continuously deferred."

**Xanadu** (Winick, Burke, Boussema, Wright, Duarte et al. 2025, arXiv:2511.06195; NeurIPS 2025 Creative AI Track; AAAI AIIDE 2025) — immersive participatory production of the Broadway musical *Xanadu* at UCLA, ~500 audience members across 7 performances, up to 65 simultaneous co-creators per show. Seven 13-foot LED "shrines" for the muses. Architecture combines GPT-style reasoning with audio/image diffusion at 30–60s latency. Four-facet framework: **audience input as offerings; performers as ritual guides; AI as instrument played by humans; reciprocity**. This is the strongest extant academic-arts framing of **AI-as-ritual-intermediary** and the closest conceptual neighbour to PostListener.

**Synthesis: what's validated.** Biometric/contextual real-time inputs (Endel) have suggestive industry validation. Voice-driven co-creation with spatial agency (Sonora) has CHI 2025 quantitative validation in trait-anxious subgroups. Ritual framing and group co-creation (Xanadu) has qualitative reporting at n≈500. Narrative/voice overlays on top of recommender output (Spotify) have commercial uptake but no public effect sizes. **Pre-experience questionnaire-as-ritual is surprisingly under-explored in the academic literature — this is the gap PostListener occupies.**

## SECTION 2 — PROPOSED 5-MINUTE FLOW

### Library architecture: 6 archetypes × 4 variations

**Recommended over alternatives.** A 6×4 architecture mirrors Pandora's broad-genome → fine-variation logic, aligns with how listeners narrate their own taste (an emotional/aesthetic *type* + a *flavour*), and maps to a clean depth-2 decision tree. The alternative 4×3×2 (genre × valence × tempo) forces orthogonal axes that listeners do not actually experience as orthogonal — valence and tempo are correlated in lived listening, and forcing them apart leaves half the cells with awkward designed-around tracks. 6×4 also preserves macro-differentiation (the archetype is the felt-personalization payload) while giving the within-archetype AVD modulation that the original hybrid architecture intended.

**Archetype candidates** (placeholder names — final naming should follow Co-Star's "biting truth" register): **The Late-Night Architect** (introspective electronic / ambient piano); **The Hearth-Keeper** (warm folk / soft Americana / slow R&B); **The Velvet Mystic** (lush orchestral / dream-pop / chamber-strings); **The Quiet Insurgent** (post-rock / minor-key indie / restrained punk); **The Slow Glow** (downtempo soul / lo-fi / chillwave); **The Sky-Seeker** (cinematic / triumphant / awe-inducing). Each has 4 variations along (era × intensity), e.g. The Late-Night Architect ∈ {2010s lo-fi ambient piano; 1980s synth-melancholy; 2020s neo-classical with field recordings; 1970s ECM-style jazz piano}.

**Each track is hand-coded across ~30 attributes** (Pandora-lite genome): GEMS-9 emotion profile, Cowen-13 secondary tags, era stylistic markers, production aesthetic (lo-fi/hi-fi, analog/digital, dry/reverberant), instrumentation seed, vocal presence/style, tempo class, dynamic profile, harmonic complexity, brightness (spectral centroid), opening signature.

### The 5-minute flow, phase by phase

The ritual arc is **arrival → labour → mirror**, with the autobiographical module deliberately **placed in the middle** (not the front). Janata 2007's 30% MEAM yield was achieved in already-receptive participants; front-loading three song prompts at second 0 hits a stressed user before parasympathetic shift, producing shallow recall and weak identity-binding. Place autobiographical recall in the calmed window after somatic onboarding, where Belfi 2018's mPFC system is most engaged.

**Phase 0 — Threshold (0:00–0:35) — *Separation rite*.**
*Mechanic:* Black screen. Single low drone (60Hz, no music yet — Bernardi 2006: silence outperforms calming music). The Admirer voice in **Caretaking register** says the listener's first name (collected at landing page), then: *"For the next sixteen minutes you are not your inbox. Place one hand on your chest. We're going to do this together. Breathe out — slowly — through your mouth."* Six-second guided exhale to a timer ring. Then: *"Once more."*
*Modality:* audio + minimal visual (drone + breath ring) + motor (hand on chest).
*Extracts:* nothing — this is somatic onboarding only.
*Ritual function:* Van Gennep separation. Threshold-statement (discontinuity). Mask-donning analogue (hand on chest). Vagal activation via extended exhale (Lehrer & Gevirtz: HRV resonance achievable in fractions of a minute). Cardiff opening-silence + breath-before-content pattern. Address-by-name engages mPFC self-system before any preference probe.
*Research grounds:* van Gennep 1909/1960; Cardiff *Forty Part Motet*; Bernardi et al. 2006; Lehrer & Gevirtz 2014; Zaccaro et al. 2018; Punchdrunk *Between Worlds* MIT report 2014; Janata 2009 mPFC self-engagement.

**Phase 1 — First Pull (0:35–2:05) — *Pairwise audio anchoring + IKEA labour*.**
*Mechanic:* 9 forced-choice audio pairs. Each pair = two 8-second clips, polar contrasts along key library axes (warm vs cold; dense vs spare; sung vs instrumental; analog vs digital; major vs modal-minor; slow vs mid; rhythmically-driving vs floating; low-register-dominant vs high-register-dominant; reverberant vs dry). User taps the one they would keep. The chosen clip *briefly continues* for ~3 seconds before fading — small reward, choice-supportive bias activation.
*Modality:* audio + motor (tap).
*Extracts:* 9-bit signal mapping to AVD axes + 3 key production-aesthetic dimensions (analog/digital, density, register). Plus implicit signals: skip rate, decision latency.
*Admirer voice:* **Present register**, sparse — only between pairs 3 and 6 a single comment ("You're choosing the warmer ones — interesting"). This is operational transparency (Buell & Norton 2011).
*Ritual function:* the *labour* phase. Each tap is an IKEA-effect deposit; nine micro-decisions justify the eventual output. Pairwise reduces cognitive load vs Likert and exploits choice-supportive bias.
*Research grounds:* Nave et al. 2018; Bradley & Terry 1952; Christiano et al. 2017; Norton et al. 2012; Mather, Shafir & Johnson 2000; Buell & Norton 2011; Saari & Eerola 2014. **Why 9 not 25:** the 24-track decision needs ≈4.6 bits; 9 well-chosen pairs supply ample signal under adaptive selection (Golbandi et al. 2010), and fatigue caps total trial budget at ~25 across all phases.

**Phase 2 — The GEMS Probe (2:05–2:55) — *Discrete-emotion capture*.**
*Mechanic:* Three 15-second emotionally-distinctive excerpts presented sequentially (a Sublimity exemplar — wonder/transcendence; a Tenderness/longing exemplar; a Tension/Power exemplar drawn to provoke a reaction in either direction). After each, a single **"Tell me what just happened"** moment — six emotion-word tiles drawn from GEMS-9 + Cowen-13 (*nostalgic, awed, tender, melancholic, defiant, peaceful*), tap any that apply (multi-select). Time pressure: tiles fade after 6 seconds.
*Modality:* audio + motor + text.
*Extracts:* this is the discrete-emotion vector that AVD cannot recover — directly captures nostalgia, awe, melancholy, tenderness, peacefulness, defiance affinities.
*Admirer voice:* between excerpts 2 and 3, in **Elevated register**: *"You're letting the sad ones land. Most people skip past them."* (This is a soft Forer probe — flattering specificity that the system will quietly verify against the actual tap pattern.)
*Ritual function:* deepens engagement; converts passive listening into avowed feeling. The time-pressure tile-fade is a Buechel & Janiszewski (2014) structured-effort move — bounded labour produces more attachment than open-ended.
*Research grounds:* Zentner et al. 2008; Coutinho & Scherer 2017; Cowen et al. 2020; Eerola & Vuoskoski 2011 (discrete vs dimensional); Furnham & Schofield 1987 (Forer specificity moderator).

**Phase 3 — Three Songs You Carry (2:55–4:10) — *Autobiographical bump module*.**
*Mechanic:* three sequential prompts, each with a single text-input field (autocomplete via a music metadata API), borrowing Rathbone's "I am…" identity framings rather than generic "favourite": **(a)** *"A song from when you became someone."* **(b)** *"A song that belongs to one specific person."* **(c)** *"The first song that felt like it was yours — not borrowed."* Each entry is followed by a 4-second pause where the user can simply type, then tap done. No need to listen to anything. Prompts are visually staged on a dim background; the Admirer voice introduces each prompt softly.
*Modality:* text + minimal audio.
*Extracts:* era (via release-year metadata of named songs); genre cluster; production aesthetic (via Spotify-API-style features on retrieved tracks); identity-binding intensity; cohort/generational position. Crucially, this is where era and instrumentation seed get their richest signal.
*Admirer voice:* **Caretaking register**. Between prompts: *"Take your time. The wrong one would feel wrong."* (Authenticity framing — the system trusts the user, which inverts the assessor-tested-subject power relation that triggers performance anxiety.)
*Ritual function:* this is the deepest engagement window — the listener is now in calm, mPFC-engaged, self-referential mode. Janata's lesion evidence (Belfi et al. 2018) tells us this is the moment when music's identity-binding power is maximal. The middle placement is deliberate: Phase 0 + Phase 1 buy parasympathetic shift; Phase 2 warms emotional vocabulary; Phase 3 cashes in.
*Research grounds:* Krumhansl & Zupnick 2013; Janata 2009; Janata, Tomic & Rakowski 2007; Belfi, Karlan & Tranel 2016, 2018; Rathbone, O'Connor & Moulin 2017; Jakubowski et al. 2020; Renwick & Woolhouse 2023; Salakka et al. 2021/2024; North & Hargreaves 1999/2000.

**Phase 4 — One True Word (4:10–4:35) — *Tempo + dial gesture*.**
*Mechanic:* a single horizontal slider — **not** a Likert dial but an aesthetic continuum. Visual: the slider's position changes the colour-saturation and tempo of an ambient pulse playing underneath. Prompt text: *"Pull this until it feels like the room you want to be in."* No labels at the ends — just a left-end and right-end ambient pulse cue (one slow + warm, one bright + propulsive). A second below: *"Tap when it's right."*
*Modality:* visual + motor + audio (live-modulated underscore).
*Extracts:* tempo class; intensity; brightness preference. Acts as a *passive sensing* moment by virtue of being unfalsifiable — the user feels they have made a precise choice when in fact the underlying signal is binary-equivalent.
*Admirer voice:* silent during gesture. After tap: *"Okay. Hold there."*
*Ritual function:* the agency-affordance (Sundar & Marathe 2010; Vaccaro et al. 2018) — even small placebo controls increase satisfaction. Combined with the live-modulated underscore, this is operational transparency *and* IKEA labour at minimal cognitive cost.
*Research grounds:* Sundar 2008 agency model; Vaccaro et al. 2018 illusion of control; Bernardi et al. 2006 tempo entrainment; Buell & Norton 2011 visible work.

**Phase 5 — The Mirror (4:35–5:00) — *Forer reflection + transition into music*.**
*Mechanic:* black background. A single named archetype appears in elegant typography — **the user's archetype + a one-word variation tag** drawn from the 6×4 library (e.g., *"The Late-Night Architect / 2010s"*). The Admirer voice in **Elevated register** reads back a **60-second Forer-balanced profile**:

> *"You appreciate music that rewards a second listen — the kind with hidden depth. You are drawn to the second half of songs more than the first. You resist anything that asks for your full attention before earning it, but you'll give an entire night to a song that does. You keep your saddest songs for cab rides home. You are moved by what other people find too quiet."*

(Three flattering claims, one mild challenge, one specific-but-unfalsifiable image — Furnham & Schofield 1987 recipe; Co-Star/16Personalities idiom.)

The user's Phase 1 pairwise picks appear faintly behind the text as visual residue — legitimacy-laundering. While the voice reads, a soft fade-up of the matched track's first second begins, sub-audible. As the voice finishes:

> *"I have something for you."*

And the track plays in full.

*Modality:* visual + audio + text.
*Extracts:* nothing further — this is the personalization-payload phase.
*Ritual function:* the *aggregation* turn that completes separation and hands the listener into the liminal phase. The named archetype is the take-home artifact (Co-Star/BuzzFeed share-loop affordance). The Forer block is the felt-personalization payload. The unbroken transition into the track preserves the affect at peak before any disconfirmation can occur.
*Research grounds:* Forer 1949; Furnham & Schofield 1987; Snyder & Shenkel 1977; Glick et al. 1989; Li 2016; Tam & Ho 2006; Sundar 2008; Norton et al. 2012; Pataranutaporn et al. 2023; Co-Star/Pattern design analyses.

### Passive sensing / uncanny element

Two recommended deployments. **(a) Time-of-day sensitivity**: the Admirer voice's specific phrasings adjust to the local clock — at 11pm–4am the voice references "this hour," at 5–8am it references "you got here early," at midday it references "the middle of the day is hard for music." This is a single conditional that produces a disproportionate uncanny effect (Co-Star/The Pattern pattern). **(b) Phase-1 latency sensing**: if the user's average decision latency in Phase 1 is >3.5s, the Admirer voice in Phase 5 includes the line *"You took your time. That's what I needed from you."* If <1.5s, it includes *"You knew what you wanted. That helped."* This is a true passive signal converted into Forer-compatible output.

### Mapping the profile to the library

Hierarchical knowledge-based decision (Burke 2002 cascade hybrid):

**Step 1 — archetype selection** via hand-engineered scoring function over (Phase 1 pairwise vector + Phase 2 emotion tile pattern). Each archetype has a hand-coded weight vector across ~12 dimensions; argmax of dot products + temperature-scaled softmax for confidence. **No stochasticity at this level** — a wrong archetype breaks the felt-personalization contract.

**Step 2 — variation selection** within archetype via cosine similarity in the genome subspace, conditioning on (Phase 3 era signal + Phase 4 tempo dial + Phase 2 secondary emotion tags). **Inject ε ≈ 0.10–0.15 stochasticity here** (Anderson et al. 2020 serendipity rationale; Zhang et al. 2012 *Auralist*). A wrong variation produces a "surprise hit" effect; a right variation feels uncannily-on; either way the felt-personalization holds because the listener has no counterfactual.

**Fallback rule.** If max archetype softmax < τ_low (≈0.25 — a non-engaged listener), default to a designer-chosen "universal" track — typically the warmest, slowest, most acoustically familiar variation in The Hearth-Keeper or The Slow Glow. This handles Sonora's trait-anxiety insight: the system should never produce a high-arousal challenging output for a user who hasn't shown engagement.

### Evaluation methodology suggestions

A **Sonora-style between-subjects design** (De La Torre et al. 2025, CHI; n=32) is the most directly applicable template. Conditions: (i) **full-ritual** (this proposed flow); (ii) **labour-stripped** (same questions, no Admirer voice, no Phase 0, no Mirror); (iii) **passive** (skip the 5 minutes — go straight to a randomly assigned track). Pre-/post-state-affect (PANAS, STAI-S), single-item felt-personalization rating ("how much did this music feel made for you?", 7-point), exit interview on the Mirror's perceived accuracy (Forer-acceptance score), 7-day follow-up retention of the archetype label and willingness-to-share. Trait-anxiety stratification per Sonora's subgroup finding (effects concentrate in moderate-to-high trait-anxiety users). Target n ≈ 60–90 to power the three-arm comparison; Prolific recruitment with music-listener screening. **Original empirical contribution available**: no peer-reviewed study has tested Forer/Barnum statements about *musical* taste — a planned subscale comparing music-taste Barnum acceptance vs personality-trait Barnum acceptance would be a clean thesis contribution.

## SECTION 3 — OPEN QUESTIONS AND DESIGN TENSIONS

These are the eight points where reasonable designers could disagree and where brainstorming should focus.

**1. Voice-throughout vs voice-bookends.** The proposed flow uses the Admirer voice across all phases. The alternative (voice only in Phase 0 and Phase 5, silent through 1–4) reduces ASMR-inhibition risk (Barratt 2017), avoids parasocial fatigue, and reserves voice for ritual-marking. Argument *for* voice-throughout: Cardiff's continuous companionship pattern, Pataranutaporn's priming evidence, operational transparency. Argument *for* bookends: clearer separation/aggregation marking, higher payoff at the Mirror, lower production cost.

**2. Autobiography front-loaded vs middle-placed.** I have argued for middle placement (post-arrival, pre-mirror) on Janata/Belfi grounds. The counterargument: front-loading the three song prompts produces a more immediately-personalized signal that the system can use to *seed* the rest of the flow (e.g., Phase 1 pairs are drawn from the same era as the named songs). This trades ritual depth for algorithmic richness. Empirical question: does the Janata 30% MEAM rate hold for stressed pre-onboarding users, or only for calmed mid-flow users?

**3. Six archetypes vs eight (or four).** The 6×4 is recommended above; eight archetypes provides finer macro-differentiation but risks ties and breaks the depth-2 decision tree; four archetypes reduces the felt-personalization signal (each user lands in a 1-in-4 bucket — too obviously generic). Six is the BuzzFeed/MBTI sweet spot for shareable archetype identity.

**4. Genre-stratified vs mood-stratified library.** The proposed archetypes are mood-stratified (with era as the variation axis). The genre-stratified alternative (4 genres × 3 valence × 2 tempo) is more orthogonal and easier to QA but, as argued, breaks the way listeners actually narrate taste. There is a hybrid worth considering: 6 mood-archetypes × 2 genre-flavours × 2 era-flavours (still 24 cells), giving genre and era explicit axes within mood.

**5. Pairwise audio vs descriptor-card vs pairwise-image.** Phase 1 uses pairwise audio. Alternatives: (a) Greenberg's original descriptor cards (textures), which are faster but lose audio specificity; (b) pairwise *images* (Co-Star aesthetic / Midjourney-style) which are even faster and may correlate well with sonic preference but are unvalidated for music; (c) a hybrid first-half audio, second-half image. Empirical question: do image-pair preferences predict track preference at useful effect sizes?

**6. Explicit vs implicit personalization labelling.** The proposed Mirror uses second-person possessive language ("your track") without overclaim. The aggressive alternative (Co-Star's "Stanford-validated AI psychometric profile") maximises authority-laundering at the cost of disconfirmation risk (Aguirre 2015 creepiness paradox; Ragot 2020 AI-label nocebo). The minimal alternative (no personalization claim at all — just the track) loses Li 2016's perceived-personalization driver. Mid-strength language (proposed) appears safest but is the least-tested point in the design.

**7. Mirror length and tone.** 60-second Forer profile vs 30-second (less risk of disconfirmation, less IKEA payoff) vs 90-second (deeper Barnum effect, higher dropout risk, harder to sustain Admirer-voice register). Tone: Co-Star's biting register (specific-but-aggressive) vs 16Personalities' affirming register (specific-and-warm). The biting register is more memorable and shareable but less compatible with the Caretaking voice register the rest of the flow establishes; tonal inconsistency at the Mirror could break the ritual frame.

**8. Stochasticity at archetype level vs only at variation level.** Proposed: ε-greedy at variation level only. Alternative: ε at archetype level too, treating the surprise of "wrong" archetype as a feature. Argument for archetype-level stochasticity: forces broader catalog use across thesis evaluation runs, and Pandora retrospectives suggest "wrong" matches sometimes feel more uncannily right than safe ones. Argument against: a wrong archetype undermines the entire felt-personalization contract and risks systematic mismatches for some user clusters.

**Two further tensions worth flagging.** **(9) Whether to ship a take-home artifact.** Co-Star/BuzzFeed strongly support shareable archetypes; thesis-evaluation considerations may favour leaving the Mirror un-shareable to avoid social-disclosure confounds. **(10) Whether to use familiar music in Phase 2.** Cowen 2020 used unfamiliar; Barrett 2010 shows nostalgia requires familiarity — there is a clean tradeoff between recoverability of nostalgia and stimulus-control rigour. A compromise: use unfamiliar audio in Phase 2 but allow familiarity to enter via Phase 3's named songs.

## Conclusion

The literature converges on five cross-cutting prescriptions for the redesign. **First, replace AVD-only with a hybrid AVD + GEMS-discrete + autobiographical-bump architecture** — AVD geometrically cannot recover the specific emotions PostListener wants to evoke, while GEMS-9 + Cowen-13 + 3 reminiscence-bump prompts can, in budget. **Second, treat the 5 minutes as a Van Gennep separation rite, not a survey** — silence-then-breath-then-name-then-threshold-statement is empirically supported as a sub-60-second body-arrival sequence, and Sleep No More's MIT analysis demonstrates that digital onboarding without somatic separation rites materially fails. **Third, deploy felt personalization through visible labour, second-person address, and Forer-balanced reflection** — Li 2016, Vaccaro 2018, and Pataranutaporn 2023 collectively establish that perception trumps algorithm; Norton 2012's IKEA effect requires visible successful completion; Forer-Barnum mechanics are durable across skeptics. **Fourth, treat the 24-track catalog as a Pandora-lite knowledge-based recommender, not a CF problem** — hand-coding 24 tracks at 30 minutes each is feasible, interpretable, and thesis-defensible; embeddings (CLAP, MERT) are overkill at this scale. **Fifth, inject ε-greedy serendipity only at variation level** — wrong archetypes break the contract, but wrong variations produce surprise-hit effects that anecdotal Pandora history and Anderson-Maystre's diversity findings both endorse.

The novel intellectual contribution PostListener can claim — and which the current literature does not occupy — is the use of a structured **pre-experience profiling-as-ritual** as the felt-personalization mechanism for generative-music output. Sonora validates voice-driven co-creation; Xanadu validates AI-as-ritual-intermediary in a group context; Liminal articulates the philosophical frame. None of them demonstrate that a **5-minute one-shot questionnaire-as-rite** can replace per-listener model fine-tuning. PostListener's thesis defensibility depends on showing exactly that. The proposed flow is designed to make that case in a single, coherent, Sonora-style between-subjects evaluation.
