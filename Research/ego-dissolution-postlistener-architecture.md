# Dissolution and return: a research architecture for PostListener's second half

**PostListener can produce a meaningful encounter with the threshold of ego dissolution through sound alone — but only if four distinct mechanisms converge in precise sequence and the return is treated as the ethical and aesthetic center of the design.** The REBUS model (Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019) provides the theoretical backbone: ego structure is maintained by precision-weighted high-level priors, and systematically removing external scaffolds that support those priors — musical coherence, gestural agency, relational mirroring, narrative continuity — produces cascading relaxation of self-structure. Christov-Moore et al. (2026) demonstrated that chills-inducing music combined with contemplative attention produces measurable ego dissolution (EDI increase, β = 6.77 for chills predicting dissolution), confirming that pharmacological intervention is not required. The honest framing: PostListener occupies the territory between deep flow and mild dissolution — briefer than Guided Imagery and Music therapy, gentler than psychedelics, more structured than meditation, more interactive than passive listening. This is enough to be meaningful. What follows is the research base, mechanism specification, technique inventory, and ethical architecture for making it land.

## The four mechanisms are distinct processes that must converge

Dissolution in PostListener is not one event but four interacting processes, each undermining a different pillar of the self-model. The research establishes that these are phenomenologically separable, targetable through different design parameters, and most powerful when choreographed to cascade.

### Ego dissolution: which components of self come apart, and in what order

Raphael Millière's taxonomy (2017, Frontiers in Human Neuroscience; elaborated 2018 with Carhart-Harris, Roseman, Trautwein & Berkovich-Ohana) identifies **six aspects of self-consciousness** that dissociate during ego dissolution: reduction in self-related thoughts, loss of narrative self-content (autobiographical identity), loss of body ownership, diminished bodily awareness, loss of spatial self-location, and changes in phenomenal richness. Critically, Millière argues for a multidimensional rather than hierarchical model — these components can dissociate independently, meaning a designer can target specific layers without requiring full dissolution.

For PostListener's browser-based audio context, three components are most accessible. The **narrative self** (self-referential thought, inner monologue) is the easiest target: immersive sound captures absorptive attention, and research consistently shows that high absorption reduces default mode network activity, which sustains self-referential processing. **Spatial self-location** is powerfully manipulated through HRTF-based spatial audio — the technology from Workstream C directly targets where the listener perceives themselves to be. **Phenomenal richness** is shaped entirely by sound design: timbral complexity, spectral density, harmonic content. The deeper layers — body ownership, minimal selfhood — are harder to reach without pharmacological support or extended practice, and PostListener should not claim to reach them.

The REBUS model provides the mechanism. The ego functions as what Carhart-Harris and Friston call an "umbrella prior" — a high-level belief ("I am a unified agent with particular characteristics") that constrains the entire predictive hierarchy. When this prior's precision-weighting is relaxed, the brain's energy landscape **flattens**, attractor basins become shallower, and bottom-up sensory information gains disproportionate influence over conscious experience. The model describes this as analogous to annealing: heating a system dissolves its crystalline structure, enabling reorganization upon cooling. The dissolution phase of PostListener is the heating; the return is the cooling into a potentially revised configuration.

The Ego Dissolution Inventory (Nour et al., 2016) provides the phenomenological targets. Its eight items cluster around three experiential dimensions: dissolution of self-boundary ("the boundary between myself and my surroundings had dissolved"), unity experience ("existing as a single awareness or consciousness"), and decreased self-importance. Each maps to specific audio design parameters. Self-boundary dissolution maps to spatial audio techniques that blur inside/outside distinctions. Unity experience maps to immersive, enveloping soundscapes that remove foreground/background hierarchy. Decreased self-importance maps to sonic vastness — sounds that communicate scale beyond the human.

The critical safety distinction comes from the 5D-ASC framework (Dittrich, 1998; Studerus et al., 2010), which separates **Oceanic Boundlessness** (positively experienced dissolution: bliss, unity, insight) from **Dread of Ego Dissolution** (negatively experienced: anxiety, loss of control, panic). Roseman et al. demonstrated that OBN positively predicts (β = 0.605) and DED negatively predicts (β = -0.649) therapeutic outcomes. PostListener must maximize OBN and minimize DED through warm emotional tone, clear temporal containment, and the voluntary/aesthetic framing that distinguishes artistic ego-loosening from pathological dissociation.

### Musical dissolution: what holds an orchestra together, and what makes it come apart

Albert Bregman's Auditory Scene Analysis (1990) identifies the perceptual cues that bind sounds into coherent streams. **Simultaneous grouping** depends on common onset synchrony, harmonicity, spatial co-location, and common fate (parallel modulation). **Sequential grouping** depends on frequency proximity, temporal proximity, continuity, and timbral similarity. The auditory system is conservative — it maintains a coherent interpretation until evidence accumulates against it. This means dissolution must be gradual to feel natural; the perceptual system will "hold on" to coherence until multiple cues simultaneously degrade.

The research yields a specific removal order for the most natural-feeling dissolution. **First**: common onset synchrony — introduce 30–80ms temporal offsets between stems, below conscious detection but enough to begin decoupling sources. **Second**: common fate — let stems stop sharing dynamic envelopes, so one crescendos while another fades. **Third**: spatial coherence — gradually scatter HRTF positions so the ensemble stops sounding co-located. **Fourth**: harmonicity — introduce micro-detuning (5–25 cents, increasing), undermining harmonic fusion. **Fifth**: timbral similarity — spectral blurring, differential processing of stems. **Last**: rhythmic structure. Lucier's "I Am Sitting in a Room" demonstrates that rhythm is the final vestige of coherent structure, persisting even after all spectral and semantic content has been absorbed into room resonance. Remove rhythm last.

Two compositional models are directly relevant. William Basinski's *The Disintegration Loops* (2002–2003) — recordings of analog tape literally crumbling during playback — demonstrates that dissolution can feel like "generative death" rather than destruction. Something emerges as something else recedes. The melody doesn't die; it transforms into the material properties of the medium. For PostListener, this means the musical dissolution should reveal something underneath — the spatial environment itself, ambient resonance, the listener's own breathing — rather than simply fading to silence. Lucier's piece makes the same point: dissolution is transmutation, not erasure. The room "speaks" through the dissolution of the voice.

The technical implementation draws on production techniques from Jon Hopkins' *Music for Psychedelic Therapy* (2021), designed explicitly for ketamine-assisted therapy sessions at Imperial College London. Hopkins' approach: beatless composition, field recordings layered with synthesizers and piano, spatial depth prioritized over melody, a clear arc from welcome through descent to peak to return. His 2024 album *Ritual* contains a track called "Dissolution" — described as "the perfect cue for letting go."

### Agency dissolution: when conducting stops working

The sense of agency is maintained by what the comparator model (Frith, Blakemore, Wolpert; elaborated by Haggard) describes as a match between efference copies (predicted sensory consequences of action) and actual sensory reafference. When the match fails — when gesture and sound diverge — agency attribution weakens. Research on temporal binding establishes precise thresholds: **0–100ms** maintains full agency, **200ms** is where conscious detection begins, **500ms** produces significant degradation, and **1000ms+** effectively decouples action from outcome.

Daniel Wegner's framework ("The Illusion of Conscious Will," 2002) identifies three conditions for the experience of causation: priority (intention precedes action), consistency (outcome matches intention), and exclusivity (no other causes are apparent). Breaking these conditions in reverse dissolves the feeling of agency. The research strongly recommends a specific ordering for graceful dissolution. **First, break exclusivity**: introduce autonomous musical behaviors that clearly aren't caused by the user — the orchestra begins to "have its own mind." This is least frustrating because it doesn't undermine the user's experience of their own gestures; it simply introduces another agent. **Second, break consistency**: gestures begin producing non-matching effects — an upward motion produces lateral spread rather than crescendo. **Last, break priority**: increase temporal delay. Breaking priority first feels like system lag, not dissolution; it must come after the other two conditions have already degraded.

The critical design insight is the **uncanny valley of agency**: partial, flickering agency feels worse than no agency at all. The transition must be monotonic — steadily decreasing responsiveness, never oscillating between responsive and unresponsive. Variable, unpredictable delay is more aversive than consistent delay. The dissolution of conducting must be a one-way fade, not a stutter.

### Relational dissolution: the Admirer withdrawing

The Admirer voice (specified in Workstream E as ElevenLabs Lily across four registers) functions as what Kohut would call a selfobject — providing mirroring, attunement, and the felt sense of being seen. Its withdrawal must be optimally frustrating in Kohut's precise sense: enough loss to signal ending, not so abrupt as to feel like abandonment. Winnicott's "good enough" object provides the parallel: the good-enough mother initially provides near-total gratification, then gradually introduces frustrations in tolerable doses, building the child's capacity for independence. The music initially responds perfectly to gesture (gratification). During dissolution, it responds less (graduated frustration). During return, it acknowledges the listener once more (repair) before withdrawing (separation).

Janet Cardiff's audio walks demonstrate that a voice companion creates profound attachment in minutes. The withdrawal of a guiding voice — one that has been intimate, present, responsive — must be handled with the care of a therapeutic termination. Abrupt silence feels like abandonment. Gradual withdrawal with a final acknowledgment feels like a dignified goodbye. The Admirer should signal the ending before it arrives, shift from mirroring to contemplative presence, and offer a closing utterance that feels like completion rather than cutoff.

## What actually works for inducing dissolution through sound

The sonic technique inventory ranges from well-evidenced to mythological. PostListener should stack techniques with genuine evidence rather than rely on any single mechanism.

### Drones and sustained tones are the primary vehicle

Drone-based composition has the strongest convergent evidence for consciousness alteration through sound. When the brain receives a sustained, unchanging pitch, it stops searching for melodic or harmonic motion. Attention redirects to micro-level phenomena: overtone shimmer, beating between partials, subtle timbral shifts. This functions identically to concentrative meditation, occupying the attentional foreground enough to suppress default mode network activity without demanding effortful processing. La Monte Young's insistence on just intonation is technically relevant: precise harmonic ratios (3/2, 5/4) produce richer beating patterns and overtone interactions than equal temperament, and Web Audio API oscillators can generate these ratios exactly. The 16-minute duration is sufficient to enter a drone-listening state; typical onset is **5–10 minutes** of sustained exposure.

### Binaural beats contribute modestly but measurably

The Garcia-Argibay et al. (2019) meta-analysis of 22 studies found a **medium, significant effect (Hedges' g = 0.45)**, with anxiety reduction in the theta/delta range showing the strongest support (g = 0.69). However, Ingendoh et al.'s 2023 systematic review of EEG evidence found only 5 of 14 studies confirmed actual brainwave entrainment — the frequency-following response is real but weak. Monaural beats and isochronic tones produce stronger cortical entrainment than binaural beats, though the evidence base is smaller. A 2025 perioperative meta-analysis found significant anxiety and pain reduction from binaural beats versus both blank audio and non-binaural audio controls, providing the strongest clinical support to date.

The practical recommendation: include binaural beats at **4–7 Hz (theta range)** using carrier tones around 300–400 Hz, embedded within drone textures rather than rhythmic music (Garcia-Argibay found embedding in music reduces efficacy). Sixteen minutes exceeds the **9–10 minute minimum** for cortical effects. Do not position binaural beats as the primary mechanism — they are one layer among many, contributing a modest but real effect.

### Field recordings have the strongest evidence base for state change

Natural sounds — especially water — have the most robust and replicated evidence for consciousness alteration through sound. Buxton et al.'s 2021 PNAS meta-analysis of 18 studies found water sounds produced the greatest positive health outcomes and birdsong was most effective for stress reduction. A Sussex University fMRI study showed natural sounds shift brain activity from inward-focused fight-or-flight patterns toward outward-focused rest-digest activity, with reduced default mode network self-referential processing. For PostListener, field recordings of water serve dual purpose: they provide the parasympathetic shift that supports positive dissolution (Oceanic Boundlessness over Dread), and they semantically communicate "environment" in a way that supports the self-boundary dissolution targeted by the EDI items.

### Shamanic drumming confirms the theta target but requires duration

The 4 Hz claim for shamanic drumming has moderate support. Maxfield (1990s) found **4.5 beats/second** elicited strong theta increases; Huels et al. (2021) found shamanic practitioners showed increased gamma power and decreased neural signal diversity during 4 Hz drumming, with altered-state scores comparable to psychedelic subjects. However, controls showed weaker effects, and most studies use **20–30 minute exposures**. At 16 minutes, PostListener is at the minimum effective threshold. The technique translates not as literal drumming but as subtle amplitude modulation of drone layers at theta frequencies — a pulse embedded in texture rather than a beat imposed on structure.

### Deep Listening and yoga nidra provide the attentional framework

Pauline Oliveros's Deep Listening offers not a technique to embed but a design philosophy. Her distinction between hearing (involuntary) and listening (voluntary, selective) translates to PostListener's arc: the Recognition and Conducting phases cultivate active listening; the Dissolution phase invites the transition from controlling attention to releasing it. Her Sonic Meditations — especially "Teach Yourself to Fly" (observe breathing → make it audible → introduce voice → slowly increase → continue until quiet) — model a gradual arc from active participation to receptive stillness that maps directly onto PostListener's conducting-to-dissolution transition.

Yoga nidra provides the most relevant evidence for short-duration guided dissolution. Fialoke et al. (2024, Scientific Reports) demonstrated that audio-guided yoga nidra reduces DMN functional connectivity within the first four minutes and activates auditory, somatosensory, and limbic regions. A 2025 RCT found **11-minute yoga nidra sessions** were as effective as 30-minute sessions for reducing stress, anxiety, and rumination — crucial evidence that PostListener's time constraint is sufficient. Lou et al. (2002) found a **65% increase in dopamine release** during yoga nidra, confirming a deeply relaxed but awake state.

### Honest limits of headphone delivery

True infrasound (below 20 Hz) cannot be delivered through consumer headphones, which typically roll off sharply below 30–40 Hz. Vic Tandy's 19 Hz "ghost frequency" effects require subwoofers or physical transducers. PostListener should work within the **40–100 Hz sub-bass range**, which most decent headphones can reproduce and which still creates a sense of physical presence through bone conduction effects. The Web Audio API imposes no software frequency limits — the constraint is entirely hardware.

## The return is not an ending but the ethical center of the design

The researcher's position — "dissolution without return is closer to trauma than aesthetic experience" — is confirmed across every research domain investigated. Victor Turner's ritual theory establishes that without proper reincorporation from liminality, the participant remains "stuck" — a condition that later trauma theorists connect directly to pathology. Willoughby Britton's research at Brown University documents that **at least 10% of contemplative practitioners** experience adverse effects requiring attention, with 58% of mindfulness program participants reporting negative-valence effects. Kahneman's peak-end rule (meta-analytic r = 0.581) proves that endings disproportionately determine how experiences are remembered. The return sequence will shape the memory of the entire 16 minutes more than any other phase.

### Immersive theater has solved this problem practically

DARKFIELD's binaural audio experiences in shipping containers (Séance, Flight, Coma) use the **return of light** as the primary re-entry mechanism — sudden restoration of visual reality after complete darkness. The container door opens and participants physically cross a threshold from the altered-state space to ordinary reality. Glen Neath's key design principle: closure must be "embedded within the story," not feel like "general housekeeping." The ending is narratively motivated, not administrative.

Punchdrunk's Sleep No More (which ran for over a decade before closing in January 2025) developed a remarkably sophisticated three-phase return: a dramatic finale (narrative closure at maximum intensity), a "walkout" (a performer physically takes the audience member's hand and escorts them back to the bar while whispering a personal farewell), and the Manderley Bar (a themed decompression space where speaking is permitted and social re-engagement begins). Felix Barrett described the entry maze as "a decompression chamber where guests acclimatise to their new world"; the exit reverses this as a recompression chamber for returning.

Janet Cardiff's audio walks demonstrate the most directly applicable technique: **gradual withdrawal of the constructed audio layer to reveal the ambient soundscape beneath it**. Cardiff uses silence deliberately — "In silence, you realise the world actually isn't silent." As her voice recedes, the listener's own environment fills the space. This is the return mechanism for PostListener: the designed sound pulls back to reveal what was always underneath — the listener's breathing, the room's ambient sound, the world outside the headphones.

### The ritual structure maps onto the dissolution arc

Van Gennep's three-phase model (separation → liminality → incorporation) provides the macro-structure, but the return itself needs internal structure. Across ceremonial traditions, reincorporation involves physical markers (touching earth, eating, washing), communal acknowledgment (telling the story, being witnessed), and identity re-establishment (receiving a new name or status). For PostListener, these translate to: sensory grounding cues (sound that suggests physical presence), relational acknowledgment (the Admirer's final words), and identity re-engagement (the return of a recognizable melodic fragment from the Recognition phase that says "this was yours").

Psychedelic integration protocols (MAPS framework) consistently emphasize that integration is not a moment but a process. The guide maintains presence, provides reassurance ("this will pass"), offers grounding through physical contact and environmental orientation, and makes space for reflection. The browser post-experience interface serves this function: not an immediate survey or share prompt, but a quiet landing page with optional reflection space.

## How the four mechanisms converge across six minutes

The thesis argument is that convergence — the simultaneous, choreographed removal of multiple self-scaffolds — produces a felt experience greater than any single mechanism could achieve alone. The REBUS model supports this: dissolving high-level priors has "implications for the functioning of the rest of the hierarchy — and indeed the integrity of the hierarchy itself." Each mechanism removes a different support for the self-model, and their combined removal produces cascading relaxation.

The ordering matters. **Agency dissolution should lead**, because Deane (2020) argues that under REBUS conditions, "the system ceases to have the sense of there being an agent which can (and should) be controlling sensory outcomes." Removing the prediction-fulfillment loop of gesture-to-sound is the most direct perturbation of the self-model available in an interactive system. **Musical dissolution follows**, removing the external cognitive scaffold (Kaelen et al., 2018: music serves as "a significant source of guidance, creating a sense of grounding") that the brain relies on for temporal prediction. **Relational dissolution** (the Admirer withdrawing) removes the mirroring function that Kohut identifies as essential for self-cohesion. **Ego dissolution** is not something PostListener directly "does" — it is the emergent consequence of the other three supports being removed. The listener's self-model relaxes because the scaffolds that maintained it are gone.

### Recommended dissolution-and-return architecture

The following architecture allocates approximately **6 minutes** (minutes 10:00–16:00) across six phases, drawing on the full research base.

**Phase 1 — Threshold (10:00–11:00).** The conducting phase begins to shift. Agency dissolution initiates by breaking Wegner's exclusivity condition: the orchestra introduces autonomous behaviors not caused by gesture. The Admirer's register shifts from Elevated to Caretaking (per Workstream E specification). Musical event density begins decreasing. A sub-bass drone layer (50–70 Hz) enters beneath the musical content, and binaural beats at 5–6 Hz (theta) begin in carrier tones around 350 Hz, embedded within the drone texture. The user notices something has changed but cannot name what. Experiential target: curiosity, slight uncertainty, the edge of surrender.

**Phase 2 — Release (11:00–12:30).** Agency dissolution deepens through breaking Wegner's consistency condition: gestures produce non-matching effects (lateral spread instead of crescendo, timbral shift instead of volume change). Musical dissolution begins in Bregman order: onset synchrony breaks (30–80ms offsets between stems), then common fate diverges (stems stop sharing dynamic envelopes). Spatial positions begin scattering via HRTF processing. The Admirer becomes less frequent, more spacious — utterances fragment, may begin to spatialize or echo. Field recordings of water emerge beneath the music, barely perceptible. The transition from "doing" to "being done to" to "just being." Experiential target: the felt boundary between flow and dissolution.

**Phase 3 — Dissolution peak (12:30–13:30).** Maximum convergence. Agency fully decouples (delay exceeds 1000ms, then gesture tracking ceases entirely). All four Bregman grouping cues are degraded: stems are temporally misaligned, spatially scattered, micro-detuned (15–25 cents), and timbrally blurred through extended reverb tails (decay times > 4 seconds) that swallow attack transients. The sound becomes texture rather than music — drone, overtone shimmer, water, breathing. The Admirer's voice reduces to breath sounds or a single sustained tone, then dissolves into the spatial field. The Admirer does not say goodbye yet — she dissolves *with* the listener, not *before* the listener. Rhythmic structure, following Lucier's principle, is the last element to fade. The spatial field becomes enveloping and undifferentiated — no foreground, no background, no clear source location. Experiential target: **60–90 seconds of being without narrative, without task, without relationship — awareness in sound.** For high-absorption listeners, this may approach the EDI's "boundary between myself and my surroundings had dissolved." For others, it will feel like deep stillness. Both are sufficient.

**Phase 4 — Return threshold (13:30–14:30).** Elements begin to reconstitute, following the inverse of the dissolution order. A pulse emerges first — rhythmic structure returns, possibly tied to a breathing rhythm (~6 breaths/minute, 0.1 Hz, which resonates with cardiac vagal tone). A single melodic thread from the Recognition phase reappears, transformed but recognizable — this is the recency-anchored memory trace that will shape how the dissolution is remembered (peak-end rule). Spatial audio re-centers: sounds migrate from scattered positions back toward a comfortable, intimate proximity. The water sounds remain but become more recognizable (rain on a window rather than abstract wash). The Admirer's voice returns — not where she left off, but in the Fading/Return register specified by Workstream E: warm, direct, acknowledging. She does not mirror; she witnesses. Something like: "You're here." Experiential target: relief, recognition, gentle re-orientation.

**Phase 5 — Homecoming (14:30–15:30).** The personalized music returns in recognizable form but simpler, warmer, slower than the original. This is the "return of the tonic" — harmonic homecoming using a plagal cadence (IV→I, the "Amen" resolution) for warmth rather than the finality of a perfect authentic cadence. Gesture tracking may return briefly and softly — a final moment of agency as a gift before departure, confirming that the listener's body still works, that action still produces response. The Admirer speaks a final acknowledgment — not a summary but a closing that honors the relational bond. The spatial audio settles into a grounded, intimate position: close, centered, present. The drone layer thins to a single sustained tone. Experiential target: arrival, warmth, the sense that something has completed.

**Phase 6 — Threshold of silence (15:30–16:00).** Sound reduces to near-silence through ritardando and diminuendo. The final audible element should be carefully chosen for the peak-end rule: a sustained tone from the listener's personalized music that slowly fades, leaving the ambient hiss of headphones and the listener's own breathing. Then: **silence**. Not abrupt — a fade into the silence that was always there beneath the sound. This 30-second silence is part of the experience. The brain continues processing sound in silence (Voisin et al. confirmed listening in silence activates auditory areas). The listener's own breathing and the room's ambient sound fill the space — and that IS the return, following Cardiff's principle.

### Post-audio integration (16:00+)

The browser screen transitions to a minimal, warm visual — not stimulating, not demanding. Text appears after 5–10 seconds of silence: "Take a moment. When you're ready, you can remove your headphones." After headphone removal: a simple reflection prompt ("What did you notice?" or "What would you like to remember?") and an optional journaling space. Grounding text: "Feel the weight of the headphones. Notice your breathing. Look around the room." No pop-ups, no social sharing prompts, no ratings. The landing page functions as Punchdrunk's Manderley Bar — a decompression space, still within the aesthetic frame but returning the listener to social/verbal reality. A downloadable audio moment or generated text serves as a transitional object in Winnicott's sense — something to carry from the experience into ordinary life.

## Ethical architecture: what makes this defensible

The dissolution design is defensible precisely because the research distinguishes aesthetic self-loosening from pathological dissociation. The key differences, established across clinical and phenomenological literature: aesthetic dissolution is **voluntary** (the participant consents, expects, and can terminate at any time), **time-bounded** (16 minutes with clear arc), **contextually framed** (as art, not therapy), **emotionally warm** (targeting Oceanic Boundlessness, not Dread), and **centrifugal** (the self expands into the environment rather than collapsing inward, as in depersonalization disorder). The browser context provides inherent meta-awareness — the listener knows where they are, knows this is a designed experience, and can close the tab.

### Required safeguards

- **Pre-experience screening**: A brief self-report screen asking about history of psychotic disorders, dissociative disorders, active PTSD, and current emotional state. This is proportionate to risk — PostListener's intensity is far below pharmacological interventions, and a brief questionnaire provides baseline protection without clinical gate-keeping.
- **Informed consent framing**: "This experience is designed to create a feeling of letting go. You will conduct, you will dissolve, you will return. Everything you experience is temporary and part of the design. You can remove your headphones or close the browser at any time." This functions simultaneously as consent, cognitive map (preventing panic during dissolution), and psychological priming (expectation of return).
- **Grounding anchors maintained throughout**: Even at peak dissolution, maintain at least one perceptible sub-bass drone as an orientation point. The physical sensation of wearing headphones serves as a constant body-awareness anchor. The listener's knowledge that this is a browser tab — not a substance, not a sealed room — provides inherent safety.
- **Post-experience grounding**: The landing page normalizes the experience ("Whatever you experienced is a normal response to this type of sound experience"), offers grounding techniques (breath awareness, environmental naming), and in a thesis/installation context, provides contact information for support if anything was disturbing.
- **Dual awareness design**: The Admirer's voice should periodically anchor the listener in the "knowing-it's-an-experience" layer, maintaining what trauma therapy calls "dual awareness" — experiencing something while knowing you are experiencing it. This is the difference between dissolution and dissociation.

### Defensible claims for thesis and funding proposal

The research supports these claims: PostListener explores the experiential territory between flow and dissolution using audio-only means. It produces a brief, mild, aesthetically meaningful encounter with self-boundary softening. Drawing on the REBUS model, it systematically removes cognitive scaffolds to investigate how this shapes subjective experience. It investigates the relationship between agency, musical structure, and self-experience in a time-bound interactive audio work. It treats the return as architecturally equal to the dissolution, grounded in ritual theory, immersive theater practice, and psychedelic integration protocols.

The research does **not** support claiming that PostListener produces full ego dissolution, has therapeutic effects, or replicates psychedelic states. These would be overclaims. The honest framing — and the stronger one for both thesis defense and funding — is that PostListener is a designed encounter with the *threshold* of dissolution, using the aesthetic means available to interactive audio art. The combination of personalization, spatial audio, gesture interaction, and AI voice creates a uniquely immersive multi-modal context that may produce effects beyond what passive audio-only listening achieves, but this claim requires the pilot testing that the ElevenLabs funding would enable.

## What the sonic means alone can and cannot reach

The research consistently indicates that **trait absorption** (measured by the Modified Telegen Absorption Scale) is the strongest individual-difference predictor of whether someone experiences ego dissolution from music. Christov-Moore et al. (2026) found absorption specifically predicted ego dissolution regardless of meditation type. This means PostListener's effects will be highly variable across listeners — some will experience genuine self-boundary softening, others will experience pleasant relaxation. Both outcomes are acceptable. The experience is designed to work at multiple depths.

A 2025 RCT on yoga nidra found 11-minute sessions as effective as 30-minute sessions, suggesting that the time constraint is not the primary bottleneck. Flotation-REST research (Pantazis & Wittmann, 2025) produced measurable afterglow effects (increased mindfulness, interoceptive awareness) in 60 minutes. If flotation achieves this in an hour, a well-designed 16-minute multi-modal audio experience — with the added dimensions of personalization, agency, and relational presence — should produce at least a mild version of the same territory. The 4-minute dissolution window is at the minimum threshold for measurable state change (Aparicio-Terrés et al., 2024, demonstrated measurable absorption shifts with 1-minute audio excerpts), and the preceding 10 minutes of Recognition and Conducting serve as the "set and setting" that the psychedelic literature identifies as the primary determinant of experience quality.

The strongest honest statement: PostListener cannot produce mystical-type experiences, lasting personality changes, or clinical therapeutic outcomes through sonic means alone. What it can produce — mild absorption, flow-to-dissolution transition, temporal distortion, boundary softening, a meaningful encounter with release and return — is genuinely worth experiencing and genuinely worth studying. The thesis contribution is not "we achieved ego dissolution through sound" but "we designed a system that maps the territory between agency and dissolution, and studied what happened when people traversed it."
